Census Demographics by DMA V7.0

Version 7.0 - Last Updated: December 2025

This document provides demographic data for the Top 50 Designated Market Areas (DMAs) in the United States. Data includes TV household counts from Nielsen 2024-2025 estimates, median household income from Census Bureau American Community Survey 2024, and market characteristics relevant to media planning and advertising. DMAs are ranked by TV households and represent geographic areas where local television viewing is measured.

----------------------------------------
DMA OVERVIEW AND METHODOLOGY
----------------------------------------

A Designated Market Area (DMA) is a group of U.S. counties that form an exclusive geographic area based on television viewing habits. There are 210 DMA regions covering the entire continental United States, Hawaii, and parts of Alaska. DMAs are defined by Nielsen and used for local television audience measurement, media planning, and advertising targeting.

Total US TV Households for 2024-25 is 125,497,100. The Top 50 DMAs cover approximately 70% of US TV households. The Nielsen Season is effective September 28, 2024 for the 2024-2025 television season. Income data is sourced from the U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey 2024.

Characteristics include stable populations, manufacturing heritage, moderate media costs, and strong sports loyalty. Total TV households is approximately 14.6 million.

Southwest Region includes 8 DMAs in the Top 50 consisting of Dallas-Fort Worth, Houston, Phoenix-Prescott, San Antonio, Austin, Denver, Las Vegas, Albuquerque-Santa Fe, and Oklahoma City markets. Characteristics include fastest population growth, high Hispanic populations, younger demographics, and lower traditional media consumption. Total TV households is approximately 15.8 million.

West Region includes 6 DMAs in the Top 50 consisting of Los Angeles, San Francisco-Oakland-San Jose, Seattle-Tacoma, Sacramento, Portland, and San Diego markets. Characteristics include highest household incomes, highest CTV adoption, progressive demographics, tech-savvy population, and high Asian American populations. KEY METRICS FOR MEDIA PLANNING
----------------------------------------

Highest Household Income DMAs are led by San Francisco-Oakland-San Jose at approximately USD 136,000 median household income, followed by Seattle-Tacoma at USD 121,000, Boston-Manchester at USD 110,000, Washington DC at USD 109,700, San Diego at USD 103,000, Austin at USD 95,000-100,000, Portland at USD 96,000, Minneapolis-Saint Paul at USD 90,000-95,000, Denver at USD 92,000, and Raleigh-Durham at USD 85,000-90,000.

Fastest Growing DMAs from 2023-2025 include Tampa-St.Petersburg which showed highest income growth at 15% increase. Other fast-growing markets include Austin, Phoenix-Prescott, Dallas-Fort Worth, Raleigh-Durham, Nashville, Salt Lake City, Jacksonville, and Charlotte. Growth is driven by Sun Belt migration, corporate relocations, and lower cost of living compared to coastal markets.

Highest Hispanic Population DMAs and key markets for Hispanic advertising include Miami-Fort Lauderdale which is majority Hispanic, Los Angeles, Houston, San Antonio, Phoenix-Prescott, Dallas-Fort Worth, Albuquerque-Santa Fe, and Orlando. These markets often require bilingual or Spanish-language media strategies.

Highest CTV Penetration DMAs are led by San Francisco-Oakland-San Jose followed by Seattle-Tacoma, Boston-Manchester, Portland, Denver, and Austin. These markets have higher cord-cutting rates, younger tech-savvy populations, and higher streaming service subscriptions.
