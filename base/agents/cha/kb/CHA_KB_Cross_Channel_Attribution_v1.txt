CROSS-CHANNEL ATTRIBUTION AND MEASUREMENT

PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document provides comprehensive guidance on cross-channel attribution methodologies measurement frameworks and analytics approaches for understanding channel contribution to marketing outcomes. Proper attribution enables informed budget allocation and optimization decisions.

ATTRIBUTION MODELS

RULE-BASED MODELS

Last Click Attribution
- Full credit to final click
- Simple implementation
- Overvalues bottom funnel
- Ignores earlier touchpoints
- Common default model

First Click Attribution
- Full credit to first touch
- Awareness channel bias
- Undervalues conversion drivers
- Discovery focus
- Brand building orientation

Linear Attribution
- Equal credit distribution
- All touchpoints valued
- Simplicity advantage
- May undervalue key touches
- Journey length sensitivity

Time Decay Attribution
- Recency weighted credit
- Conversion proximity value
- Progressive credit reduction
- Configurable decay rate
- Hybrid approach option

Position-Based Attribution
- First and last emphasis
- U-shaped distribution
- Bookend touchpoint value
- Middle touch discounting
- Customizable weights
