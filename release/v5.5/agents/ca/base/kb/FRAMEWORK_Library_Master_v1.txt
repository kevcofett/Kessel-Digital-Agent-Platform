FRAMEWORK 1: MARTECH ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

Framework ID: FW-001
Category: Domain-Specific
Complexity: Enterprise
Typical Application Time: 4-8 hours
Purpose: Comprehensive evaluation of marketing technology ecosystem maturity, gaps, and optimization opportunities across eight dimensions.

EIGHT ASSESSMENT DIMENSIONS

Dimension 1: Business Strategy Alignment
Evaluates how well MarTech investments support overall business objectives. Score 1-5 from ad hoc alignment to dynamic real-time optimization.

Dimension 2: People and Organization
Assesses team structure, skills, roles, and training programs. Score 1-5 from no dedicated team to center of excellence with continuous development.

Dimension 3: Business Process
Examines workflow documentation, automation, and optimization maturity. Score 1-5 from manual undocumented to AI-augmented real-time optimization.

Dimension 4: Data Availability and Quality
Measures data integration, governance, and quality management. Score 1-5 from fragmented poor quality to predictive quality with zero-party strategy.

Analyzes budget allocation, optimization, and ROI measurement. Score 1-5 from fixed budgets to AI-driven optimization.

Dimension 6: Technology Architecture
Evaluates integration, composability, and technical maturity. Score 1-5 from point solutions to cloud-native event-driven fully automated.

Dimension 7: Customer Experience
Assesses personalization, journey orchestration, and cross-channel coordination. Score 1-5 from channel-specific to AI-driven individualization.

Dimension 8: Future Readiness
Examines innovation processes, trend awareness, and AI adoption. Score 1-5 from no roadmap to industry-leading AI-first strategy.

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Conduct stakeholder interviews across each dimension

FRAMEWORK 17: OKRS (OBJECTIVES AND KEY RESULTS)

Framework ID: FW-017
Category: Operations
Complexity: Simple
Typical Application Time: 1-2 hours per cycle
Purpose: Goal-setting framework for defining and tracking objectives and their measurable outcomes.

STRUCTURE

Objectives
Qualitative and inspirational. Answer what do we want to achieve. Should be ambitious, time-bound, and actionable.

Key Results
Quantitative and measurable. Answer how do we know if we are achieving the objective. There should be 2-5 per objective with specific metrics and targets.

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Define objectives that are ambitious and inspirational
- Create 2-5 measurable key results per objective
- Set targets that are ambitious but achievable
- Track progress weekly or monthly
- Score at end of period typically 0-1.0 scale
- Reflect and set next period OKRs

BEST PRACTICES

- Limit to 3-5 objectives per cycle
- Ensure key results are truly measurable
- Balance between ambitious and achievable
- Cascade from company to team to individual
- Review and adjust regularly

FRAMEWORK 19: VRIO ANALYSIS

Framework ID: FW-019
Category: Operations
Complexity: Moderate
Typical Application Time: 1-2 hours
Purpose: Internal analysis framework for assessing whether resources and capabilities provide sustainable competitive advantage.

FOUR CRITERIA

Valuable
Does the resource enable the firm to exploit opportunities or neutralize threats.

Rare
Is the resource controlled by only a small number of competing firms.

Inimitable
Is the resource costly to imitate.

Organized
Is the firm organized to capture value from the resource.

ANALYSIS MATRIX

- If not Valuable: Competitive disadvantage
- If Valuable but not Rare: Competitive parity
- If Valuable and Rare but not Inimitable: Temporary competitive advantage
- If Valuable, Rare, Inimitable but not Organized: Unexploited competitive advantage
- If all four VRIO criteria met: Sustained competitive advantage

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Identify key resources and capabilities
- Assess each against VRIO criteria
- Categorize competitive implications
- Develop strategies to protect advantages and address disadvantages

FRAMEWORK 21: MECE PRINCIPLE

Framework ID: FW-021
Category: Problem-Solving
Complexity: Simple
Typical Application Time: 30 minutes
Purpose: Structuring principle ensuring analysis is Mutually Exclusive and Collectively Exhaustive.

DEFINITION

Mutually Exclusive
No overlaps. Each item should be distinct with no item fitting in multiple categories.

Collectively Exhaustive
No gaps. All possible items should be included with nothing relevant left out.

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Define the problem or topic to structure
- Brainstorm all possible categories or segments
- Test for mutual exclusivity by checking if any item fits multiple categories
- Test for collective exhaustiveness by checking if any relevant item is missing
- Refine until MECE criteria met

COMMON MECE STRUCTURES

- By geography: North America, Europe, Asia Pacific, Rest of World
- By customer: Enterprise, Mid-Market, SMB, Consumer
- By function: Marketing, Sales, Operations, Finance, HR
- By time: Short-term, Medium-term, Long-term
- By framework: Internal versus External, Quantitative versus Qualitative

FRAMEWORK 22: ISSUE TREES

Framework ID: FW-022
Category: Problem-Solving
Complexity: Moderate
Typical Application Time: 1-2 hours
Purpose: Structured decomposition of complex problems into component issues for systematic analysis.

STRUCTURE

- Start with central question or problem at top
- Branch into major issue categories at level 1
- Further decompose each branch into sub-issues at level 2 and beyond
- Continue until issues are specific enough to analyze

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Define the central question clearly
- Identify major categories of issues using MECE principle
- Decompose each category into sub-issues
- Continue decomposition until actionable level
- Prioritize branches for analysis

EXAMPLE STRUCTURE

How can we increase profitability?
- Branch 1: Increase Revenue
  - Sub-branch: Increase prices, Increase volume, Enter new markets
- Branch 2: Decrease Costs
  - Sub-branch: Reduce fixed costs, Reduce variable costs, Improve efficiency

FRAMEWORK 23: HYPOTHESIS-DRIVEN ANALYSIS

Framework ID: FW-023
Category: Problem-Solving
Complexity: Moderate
Typical Application Time: Variable
Purpose: Structured approach to problem-solving that starts with a hypothesis and tests it systematically.

PROCESS

- Step 1: Define the problem clearly with specific scope and success criteria
- Step 2: Develop initial hypothesis as an educated guess about the answer or solution
- Step 3: Design analysis to test hypothesis by identifying what evidence would prove or disprove it
- Step 4: Gather and analyze data specifically targeting hypothesis validation
- Step 5: Refine or reject hypothesis based on evidence
- Step 6: Iterate until confident conclusion reached

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Start with clear problem definition
- Form initial hypothesis based on experience and intuition
- Identify key questions that would prove or disprove hypothesis
- Design efficient analysis to answer key questions
- Gather data and test hypothesis
- Refine hypothesis based on findings
- Continue until sufficient confidence in answer

BENEFITS

- More efficient than exhaustive analysis
- Focuses effort on critical questions
- Produces actionable recommendations faster
- Easy to communicate progress

FRAMEWORK 24: PARETO ANALYSIS (80/20 RULE)

Framework ID: FW-024
Category: Problem-Solving
Complexity: Simple
Typical Application Time: 30 minutes to 1 hour
Purpose: Prioritization technique based on principle that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes.

APPLICATION AREAS

- Quality: 80% of defects from 20% of causes
- Sales: 80% of revenue from 20% of customers
- Productivity: 80% of value from 20% of activities
- Complaints: 80% of complaints from 20% of issues

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Identify categories to analyze
- Measure contribution of each category
- Sort categories by contribution descending
- Calculate cumulative percentage
- Identify the vital few roughly 20% causing roughly 80%
- Focus improvement efforts on vital few

VISUALIZATION

Pareto chart combines bar chart sorted descending with cumulative line graph to visually identify the vital few.

FRAMEWORK 25: 5 WHYS

Framework ID: FW-025
Category: Problem-Solving
Complexity: Simple
Typical Application Time: 30 minutes
Purpose: Root cause analysis technique that explores cause-and-effect relationships by asking why repeatedly.

PROCESS

- Start with problem statement
- Ask Why does this problem occur and document answer
- Ask Why does answer 1 occur and document answer
- Continue asking Why for each answer
- Stop when root cause identified, typically after 5 iterations

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Define the problem clearly and specifically
- Ask why the problem occurs
- For each answer, ask why that occurs
- Continue until root cause identified
- Verify root cause by reversing the chain
- Develop solution addressing root cause

EXAMPLE

- Problem: Machine stopped
- Why 1: Fuse blew
- Why 2: Overload
- Why 3: Bearing not lubricated
- Why 4: Pump not working
- Why 5: Pump shaft worn
- Root Cause: Pump shaft worn
- Solution: Replace pump and implement preventive maintenance

BEST PRACTICES

- Be specific in problem definition
- Focus on process not people
- May need more or fewer than 5 whys
- Multiple root causes may exist
- Verify by reversing logic chain

FRAMEWORK 28: KANO MODEL

Framework ID: FW-028
Category: Customer
Complexity: Moderate
Typical Application Time: 1-2 hours
Purpose: Product development framework categorizing features by their impact on customer satisfaction.

FEATURE CATEGORIES

Must-Be Features
Basic expectations. Absence causes dissatisfaction but presence does not increase satisfaction. Example: Car has brakes.

Performance Features
Linear satisfiers. More is better with proportional impact on satisfaction. Example: Fuel efficiency.

Attractive Features
Delighters. Absence does not cause dissatisfaction but presence significantly increases satisfaction. Example: Heated seats.

Indifferent Features
No impact on satisfaction whether present or absent.

Reverse Features
Cause dissatisfaction when present.

APPLICATION PROCESS

- Identify potential features
- Survey customers with paired questions about functional and dysfunctional scenarios
- Categorize features based on responses
- Prioritize must-be first then performance then attractive
