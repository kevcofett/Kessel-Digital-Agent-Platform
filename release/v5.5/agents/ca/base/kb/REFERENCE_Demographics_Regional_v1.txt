Census Demographics Regional Reference V7.0

Version 7.0 - Last Updated: December 2025

This document provides state-level demographic summaries and regional population distributions for the United States. Data is sourced from the U.S. Census Bureau Vintage 2024 Population Estimates and American Community Survey 2024. Information is organized by Census Region and includes population totals, median household income, urban/suburban/rural distributions, and key demographic characteristics for media planning and market analysis.

----------------------------------------
NATIONAL OVERVIEW
----------------------------------------

Total US Population as of July 2024 is 340.1 million. The annual growth rate is 0.98% which is the highest since 2001. The population growth driver is net international migration which accounted for 84% of the 3.3 million population increase. National Median Household Income for 2024 is USD 81,604. Urban population is approximately 279 million representing 82% of total. Rural population is approximately 56 million representing 18% of total. For self-identification, 52% of households describe their neighborhood as suburban, 27% describe it as urban, and 21% describe it as rural.

----------------------------------------
NORTHEAST REGION
----------------------------------------

The Northeast region includes Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, and Vermont. This region has the second-highest median incomes and oldest median age among Census regions.

Population Overview for Northeast

Total population for 2024 is 57.8 million. Growth trend is rebounding after years of decline with 0.1% to 1.0% growth depending on city size. Net international migration for 2024 was positive 567,420. Net domestic migration for 2024 was negative 192,109 which is improving from negative 278,245 in 2023. White population share is 64% which is second highest after Midwest.

State-Level Data for Northeast

NEW YORK has a population of 19.5 million with median household income of USD 81,000-85,000. Key characteristics include highest Gini index for income inequality, largest metro in nation, diverse urban center, and financial services hub.

PENNSYLVANIA has a population of 12.9 million with median household income of USD 73,000-77,000. Key characteristics include healthcare and education hubs, aging population, Philadelphia and Pittsburgh metros, and manufacturing heritage.

NEW JERSEY has a population of 9.3 million with median household income of USD 99,000 or higher which is among highest states. Key characteristics include high income suburban state, pharma and finance industries, and NYC metro spillover.

MASSACHUSETTS has a population of 7.0 million with median household income of USD 113,900 which is among highest states. Key characteristics include education and biotech hub, highly educated workforce, and Boston metro dominates.

CONNECTICUT has a population of 3.6 million with median household income of USD 90,000 or higher. Key characteristics include insurance and finance, wealthy suburbs, and proximity to NYC.

NEW HAMPSHIRE has a population of 1.4 million with median household income of USD 90,000 or higher. Key characteristics include lowest poverty rate at 7.2%, no sales or income tax, and Boston commuter population.

MAINE, RHODE ISLAND, and VERMONT have a combined population of approximately 3.3 million. Key characteristics include Vermont being least urban state at 35.1% urban, tourism economies, aging populations, and smaller markets.

----------------------------------------
SOUTH REGION
----------------------------------------

The South region includes Alabama, Arkansas, Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maryland, Mississippi, North Carolina, Oklahoma, South Carolina, Tennessee, Texas, Virginia, West Virginia, and the District of Columbia. This is the most populous and fastest-growing region.

Population Overview for South

Total population for 2024 is approximately 133 million making it the largest region. It is the only region with positive net domestic migration. Key growth states are Texas with positive 562,941 and Florida with positive 467,347 leading numeric gains. Fastest growing is District of Columbia at 2.2% growth.

State-Level Data for South Tier 1 (Major Markets)

TEXAS has a population of 31.3 million making it the second most populous state with median household income of USD 75,000-80,000. Key characteristics include fastest major state growth, diverse economy spanning energy, tech, and healthcare, high Hispanic population, and multiple major metros including Dallas, Houston, Austin, and San Antonio.

FLORIDA has a population of 23.4 million making it the third most populous state with median household income of USD 70,000-75,000 although Tampa showed 15% income growth in 2024. Key characteristics include being the fastest growing state at 3.37% annual, retirement destination, tourism hub, high Hispanic population, and no state income tax.

GEORGIA has a population of 11.0 million with median household income of USD 75,000-80,000. Key characteristics include Atlanta dominates state economy, significant African American population, film industry growth, and corporate headquarters hub.

NORTH CAROLINA has a population of 10.8 million with median household income of USD 70,000-75,000. Key characteristics include fast-growing Research Triangle and Charlotte, banking hub, universities, and diversifying economy.

VIRGINIA has a population of 8.7 million with median household income of USD 87,000-92,000. Key characteristics include government contractors, high income Northern Virginia suburbs, and military presence.

MARYLAND has a population of 6.2 million with median household income of USD 98,000 or higher which is among highest states. Key characteristics include high income state, DC suburbs, biotech and healthcare, and federal employment.

DISTRICT OF COLUMBIA has a population of 702,000 with median household income of USD 109,707 which is highest in the nation. Key characteristics include federal government hub, highest incomes, highly educated, and unique urban market.

State-Level Data for South Tier 2

TENNESSEE has a population of 7.1 million with median household income of USD 65,000-70,000. Key characteristics include fast-growing Nashville, healthcare hub, no state income tax, and music industry.

SOUTH CAROLINA has a population of 5.4 million with median household income of USD 63,000-67,000. Key characteristics include growing from Charlotte spillover, tourism, BMW manufacturing, and retirement destination.

LOUISIANA has a population of 4.6 million with median household income of USD 58,000-62,000. Key characteristics include highest poverty rate at 18.7%, energy sector, New Orleans tourism, and unique culture.

KENTUCKY has a population of 4.5 million with median household income of USD 60,000-65,000. Key characteristics include Louisville metro, bourbon industry, and automotive manufacturing.

ALABAMA, ARKANSAS, MISSISSIPPI, OKLAHOMA, and WEST VIRGINIA have a combined population of approximately 16.5 million. Key characteristics include lower income states with Mississippi having lowest median income at USD 59,127, West Virginia being the only state with population decline, and energy and manufacturing economies.

MIDWEST REGION
----------------------------------------

The Midwest region includes Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, North Dakota, Ohio, South Dakota, and Wisconsin. This region has the highest proportion of White Americans and stable but slow population growth.

Population Overview for Midwest

Total population for 2024 is 69.6 million. Growth rate for 2024 is 0.6% representing positive 410,000. Net international migration is positive 406,737. Net domestic migration is negative 49,214 showing outmigration to Sun Belt. White population share is 74.6% which is highest of any region. Non-Hispanic White share is 73% which is highest of any region. Future projection shows expected population decline by 2030-2040.

State-Level Data for Midwest Tier 1

ILLINOIS has a population of 12.5 million with population gain in 2024 of positive 67,899 which is largest in region. Median household income is USD 78,000-82,000. Key characteristics include Chicago dominates, diverse economy, significant outmigration to Sun Belt, and fiscal challenges.

OHIO has a population of 11.8 million with population gain in 2024 of positive 59,270. Median household income is USD 66,000-70,000. Key characteristics include multiple metros at Cleveland, Columbus, and Cincinnati, healthcare hub, and Intel investment in Columbus.

MICHIGAN has a population of 10.0 million with median household income of USD 68,000-72,000. Key characteristics include automotive industry, Detroit revitalization, Ann Arbor tech growth, and tourism in north.

MINNESOTA has a population of 5.7 million with median household income of USD 85,000-90,000 showing 4.9% decline since 2019. Key characteristics include corporate headquarters hub hosting Target, Best Buy, and 3M, highly educated, and Twin Cities metro dominates.

State-Level Data for Midwest Tier 2

INDIANA has a population of 6.9 million with median household income of USD 67,000-71,000. Key characteristics include Indianapolis metro, pharmaceutical hub with Eli Lilly, manufacturing, and affordable cost of living.

MISSOURI has a population of 6.2 million with median household income of USD 65,000-69,000. Key characteristics include St.Louis and Kansas City metros, central location, and agricultural economy.

WISCONSIN has a population of 5.9 million with median household income of USD 72,000-76,000. Key characteristics include Milwaukee metro, manufacturing heritage, dairy industry, and Madison university town.

IOWA, KANSAS, NEBRASKA, NORTH DAKOTA, and SOUTH DAKOTA have a combined population of approximately 10.3 million. Key characteristics include agricultural economies with North Dakota at 1.0% and Nebraska at 0.9% being fastest growing in region, small metros, and lower costs of living.

----------------------------------------
WEST REGION
----------------------------------------

The West region includes Alaska, Arizona, California, Colorado, Hawaii, Idaho, Montana, Nevada, New Mexico, Oregon, Utah, Washington, and Wyoming. This region has the highest diversity and includes the most populous state California.

Population Overview for West

Total population for 2024 is 80.0 million. Growth rate for 2024 is 0.9% representing positive 688,000. Net international migration is positive 667,794. Net domestic migration is negative 170,000 showing outmigration from California. White population share is 51.9% which is lowest of any region. Non-Hispanic White share is 47.1% meaning White population is a minority in region.

State-Level Data for West Tier 1

CALIFORNIA has a population of 39.5 million making it the most populous state at 11.4% of US. Population gain for 2024 is positive 232,570. Median household income is USD 91,000-95,000. Key characteristics include tech hub, entertainment industry, significant domestic outmigration offset by international immigration, highest diversity, and multiple major metros.

WASHINGTON has a population of 7.9 million with median household income of USD 90,000-95,000. Key characteristics include tech hub hosting Amazon and Microsoft, Seattle dominates, high incomes, and no state income tax.

ARIZONA has a population of 7.5 million with population gain in 2024 of positive 109,357. Median household income is USD 72,000-76,000. Key characteristics include fast-growing Phoenix metro, retirement destination, semiconductor manufacturing growth, and significant Hispanic population.

COLORADO has a population of 5.9 million with median household income of USD 87,000-92,000 making it the highest landlocked state. Key characteristics include Denver dominates, tech growth, outdoor lifestyle, legal cannabis market, and young educated population.

State-Level Data for West Tier 2

OREGON has a population of 4.2 million with median household income of USD 78,000-82,000. Key characteristics include Portland metro dominates, tech presence from Intel, sustainability focus, and no sales tax.

NEVADA has a population of 3.2 million with growth rate of 1.7% which is second fastest in region. Median household income is USD 70,000-75,000. Key characteristics include Las Vegas tourism and gaming, no state income tax, and California migration destination.

UTAH has a population of 3.4 million with growth rate of 1.8% which is fastest in region. Median household income is USD 87,000-92,000. Key characteristics include Silicon Slopes tech growth, youngest median age, LDS population influence, and outdoor recreation.

NEW MEXICO has a population of 2.1 million with median household income of USD 58,000-62,000. Key characteristics include significant Hispanic and Native American populations, government and military including Los Alamos and Sandia, and film industry growth.

IDAHO, MONTANA, WYOMING, ALASKA, and HAWAII have a combined population of approximately 5.5 million. Key characteristics include Idaho at 8.3% and Montana at 7.3% having largest income increases since 2019, Wyoming at negative 5.4% having largest income decline, Wyoming having smallest state population at 581,000, and Hawaii being a unique island market.

URBAN, SUBURBAN, AND RURAL DISTRIBUTION
----------------------------------------

Population by Settlement Type

The Census Bureau defines urban areas as densely settled cores with at least 2,000 housing units or 5,000 population. Rural encompasses all territory not included in urban areas. Suburban is not an official Census category but reflects how Americans self-identify their neighborhoods.

Urban core counties contain 98 million people representing approximately 29%. Suburban and small metro areas contain 175 million representing approximately 51%. Rural counties contain 46 million representing approximately 14%. Census-defined urban areas contain 279 million at 82%. Census-defined rural areas contain 56 million at 18%.

Growth Trends by Settlement Type

Urban counties have grown at roughly the national rate of 13% since 2000. Suburban and small metro areas have grown more briskly. Rural counties have lagged significantly with half having fewer residents now than in 2000. Suburban growth is outpacing urban and rural since 2000. Urban growth is matching national average at 13% since 2000. The rural trend shows 50% of rural counties have fewer residents than 2000.

Demographics by Settlement Type

Urban diversity shows people of color make up over 50% of major urban areas according to 2020 Census. Rural demographics show White residents make up 76% of rural US population. There is complexity in that 55.7% of Census rural population actually lives within metropolitan statistical areas.

Most and Least Urban States

Most urban states with over 90% urban population include California, New Jersey, Nevada, Florida, and Hawaii. Least urban is Vermont at 35.1% urban which is less than half national average. Other low urban states under 50% urban include Maine, West Virginia, Mississippi, and Montana.

INCOME DISTRIBUTION BY STATE
----------------------------------------

Highest Income States for 2024

The top income states are concentrated on the coasts with Colorado being the highest-earning landlocked state at ninth nationally.

District of Columbia leads at USD 109,707. Massachusetts follows at USD 113,900. New Jersey is at USD 99,000 or higher. Maryland is at USD 98,000 or higher. Other coastal states including California, Connecticut, and Washington are at least USD 99,000. Colorado at USD 97,113 is highest landlocked. Utah follows at USD 96,658.

Lowest Income States for 2024

Mississippi at USD 59,127 is lowest. West Virginia is USD 55,000-60,000. Arkansas is USD 58,000-62,000. Louisiana is USD 58,000-62,000. The income gap is nearly USD 51,000 difference between DC and Mississippi.

Largest Income Changes from 2019-2024

Largest increases are Idaho at positive 8.3% and Montana at positive 7.3%. Largest declines are Wyoming at negative 5.4% and Minnesota at negative 4.9%. Overall trend shows 30 states increased while 20 states plus DC declined since 2019.
