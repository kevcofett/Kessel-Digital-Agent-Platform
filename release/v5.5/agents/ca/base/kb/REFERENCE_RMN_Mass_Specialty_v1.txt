Retail Media Network Reference Guide - Mass and Specialty Retail V7.0

Version 7.0 - Last Updated: December 2025

This document provides comprehensive reference data for major Retail Media Networks in mass merchandise, consumer electronics, pharmacy, and home improvement verticals. These platforms enable brands to reach shoppers through first-party purchase data, closed-loop measurement, and advertising placements across online, in-store, and offsite channels.

----------------------------------------
TARGET ROUNDEL
----------------------------------------

Overview

Roundel is Targets retail media business, launched in 2007 and grown to more than 800 team members across the US. The platform generates nearly USD 2 billion of value for Target including ad revenue and cost offsets, with expectations to double in the next five years. Target reported USD 163 million in advertising revenue in Q1 2025, up 25.4% year over year, with full year 2024 revenue reaching USD 649 million. Roundel processes billions of shopper signals daily including website visits, in-store activity, and off-platform ad engagement, driving more than 250 million visits to Target properties in 2024.

Technical Requirements

Self-Service Platform: Roundel Media Studio for building and managing Target Product Ads with full inventory access

DSP Integrations: Precision Plus integrates with Google, Meta, Pinterest, and The Trade Desk for offsite campaigns

Data Foundation: First-party data from Target Circle loyalty program and real-time shopping behavior

Identity Solutions: Target shopper graph linking online and in-store purchase behavior

Measurement: Enhanced measurement showing campaign performance with price transparency distinguishing working media from fees

Capabilities

Onsite: Target Product Ads with personalized placements delivering estimated 10% incremental scale and average 4X ROAS versus search page placement

Precision Plus: AI-powered offsite buying solution optimizing campaigns for awareness, consideration, and conversion goals

Publisher Network: Over 150 premium publishers including New York Times, AMC Networks, Hearst, BuzzFeed, USA Today, NBCUniversal, Pinterest, PopSugar

CTV: Connected TV ads with shoppable QR codes enabling direct cart additions from ads

In-Store (2025): Testing new in-store ad experiences including demos, sampling, and digital screens

Scale: Works with more than 2,000 vendors including Apple and Mars Wrigley, with 30%+ of partner spend on offsite properties in 2024

Pricing Model

Target Product Ads: No DSP or access fees, media dollars work directly toward results

Offsite/Precision Plus: CPM-based with enhanced price transparency

Self-Service: Available through Roundel Media Studio

Managed Service: Available for larger campaigns and brand partnerships

Best Use Cases

Target Roundel is ideal for brands seeking to reach Targets valuable shopper base with AI-powered optimization across onsite and offsite channels. The platform excels at driving strong ROAS with personalized placements averaging 4X returns, shoppable CTV advertising with direct-to-cart functionality, and premium publisher reach through extensive offsite network. Particularly strong for CPG, household goods, and apparel brands aligned with Targets core shopper demographics.

----------------------------------------
BEST BUY ADS
----------------------------------------

Overview

Best Buy Ads is the retail media network of Best Buy, reaching more than 100 million loyalty members with the ability to track approximately 93% of transactional revenue back to a customer ID. In September 2025, Best Buy held its first-ever Ads Showcase called We Got Next, announcing major platform expansions. The platform has served over 600 advertisers across 3,000 campaigns, with an in-house creative department operating from a 70,000-square-foot studio in Minneapolis.

Technical Requirements

Self-Service Platform: My Ads platform with roadmap for full-funnel self-service buying over next 2-3 years

DSP Integrations: The Trade Desk, DV360, and Yahoo DSP for offsite programmatic

Data Foundation: 93% transactional revenue linked to customer ID across 100+ million members

AI Platform: AI and data science powering targeting and optimization

Creative Services: In-house 70,000-square-foot creative studio available

Capabilities

Store Takeover Packages: Premium in-store placements including window displays, entrances, checkout counters, TV walls, PC monitor displays, and interactive screens

Social+: Exclusive Meta (Facebook and Instagram) audience targeting using Best Buy first-party data with plans to expand to other platforms

Offsite: Programmatic access through Trade Desk, DV360, and Yahoo DSP

Sports Partnerships: Official Home Entertainment Retailer of the NFL, reaching shoppers 26% more likely to be sports enthusiasts

Data Collaboration: Open approach to data collaboration with other retailers and RMNs

Non-Endemic Access: Store Takeover packages designed to attract large non-endemic advertisers

Pricing Model

In-Store: Custom pricing for Store Takeover packages

Social+: CPM-based through Best Buy Ads

Offsite: CPM-based through DSP partnerships

Market Context: Retail media networks expected to account for USD 129 billion in US ad spend by 2028

Best Use Cases

Best Buy Ads is ideal for consumer electronics brands and non-endemic advertisers seeking to reach high-value tech enthusiasts. The platform excels at in-store takeover experiences for product launches and brand moments, reaching sports enthusiasts through NFL partnership, and social media targeting through Social+ Meta integration. Particularly strong for brands wanting to influence high-consideration purchases in consumer electronics, appliances, and technology categories.

CVS MEDIA EXCHANGE (CMX)
----------------------------------------

Overview

CVS Media Exchange (CMX) is the retail media network of CVS Pharmacy, celebrating its fifth anniversary in August 2025. The platform provides access to 90 million addressable consumers through the ExtraCare loyalty program, spanning beauty, health, wellness, and personal care categories. CMX captures not only purchase data but frequency and product combinations. Notably, 52% of CVS customers who engage online make an in-store purchase within 48 hours, demonstrating strong omnichannel behavior.

Technical Requirements

Clean Room: Clean room solution for secure data collaboration with advertising partners

DSP Integration: The Trade Desk integration for offsite programmatic

Data Foundation: 90+ million ExtraCare loyalty members with health, beauty, and wellness purchase data

Identity Solutions: First-party purchase data linked to loyalty program

Measurement Focus: Attribution, incrementality, and transparency as core strategy elements

Capabilities

Onsite: Sponsored Brand Ads launched April 2025 with ROAS trending above USD 8, sponsored search, on-site display

Offsite: Programmatic display, CTV, video, and audio through The Trade Desk partnership

Social: Social media channel advertising

In-Store: In-store advertising placements

Reddit Partnership: June 2025 deal combining 90 million loyalty members with Reddits 108 million daily active users through clean room for health-focused community targeting

Categories Tested: Beauty, personal care, consumer health and wellness

Pricing Model

Sponsored Ads: CPC and CPM-based auction pricing

Offsite: CPM-based through programmatic partnerships

Market Context: US retail media market projected to reach over USD 62 billion in 2025

Best Use Cases

CVS Media Exchange is ideal for health, beauty, wellness, and personal care brands seeking to reach pharmacy shoppers with strong omnichannel behavior. The platform excels at targeting based on actual health and wellness purchase patterns rather than inferred interest, reaching engaged consumers through Reddit partnership for health-focused communities, and driving strong ROAS with Sponsored Brand Ads trending above USD 8. Particularly strong for OTC healthcare, beauty, and personal care brands.

WALGREENS ADVERTISING GROUP (WAG)
----------------------------------------

Overview

Walgreens Advertising Group (WAG) launched in 2020 and has grown to become one of the fastest growing retail media networks in the US. The platform leverages data from the nations second-largest loyalty program with over 101 million myWalgreens members shopping online and across approximately 8,000 stores nationwide. Following Walgreens transition to a private company in 2025, WAG continues expanding its AI-powered retail media ecosystem.

Technical Requirements

Clean Room: LiveRamp-powered clean room solution for secure data collaboration and audience insights

AI Platform: AI and machine learning for personalized offer delivery through Rokt partnership

Data Foundation: 101+ million myWalgreens loyalty members with billions of signals

Identity Solutions: First-party purchase data across pharmacy, retail, and photo categories

Integration Partners: LiveRamp for data collaboration, Rokt for AI-powered personalization

Capabilities

Onsite: Search, display, and sponsored product advertising on Walgreens.com

Order Confirmation: December 2025 Rokt partnership enabling non-endemic brand ads on order confirmation page

Offsite: Programmatic display and video through partner integrations

In-Store: Advertising across approximately 8,000 US stores

Unique Audiences: Access to pharmacy, retail, and photo audiences

Non-Endemic Expansion: Rokt partnership opens platform to non-endemic advertisers

Pricing Model

Onsite: CPC and CPM-based pricing

Offsite: CPM-based through programmatic

Market Context: Retail media investments expected to exceed USD 74 billion by 2026

Best Use Cases

Walgreens Advertising Group is ideal for health, wellness, personal care, and CPG brands seeking pharmacy retail media scale with 101+ million loyalty members. The platform excels at reaching consumers with pharmacy and health purchase data for targeting, AI-powered personalization through Rokt partnership, and clean room data collaboration through LiveRamp. Particularly strong for OTC healthcare, beauty, household, and CPG brands wanting access to high-frequency pharmacy shoppers.

HOME DEPOT ORANGE APRON MEDIA
----------------------------------------

Overview

Orange Apron Media is the retail media network of The Home Depot, rebranded from Retail Media+ which launched in 2018. The platform reaches 198 million individual customers online and across 2,340 stores, with the website attracting 3.5 billion visits annually. Advertisers receive at least 2X return on ad spend on average, with shoppers spending 28% more per visit and being 26% more likely to convert after clicking an ad. The Orange Halo Effect study showed 36% increase in purchase intent and 75% improvement in ad recall on Meta.

Technical Requirements

Self-Service Platform: Orange Access powered by Vantage for planning, activation, optimization, and reporting

DSP Integration: Yahoo DSP partnership for offsite reach to Home Depot audiences

Data Foundation: First-party shopper data from 198 million customers

Clean Room: Data clean room solution for measurement

Media Partners: Telemundo, Univision, and expanding publisher network

Capabilities

Onsite: Top-of-page banners, sponsored product carousels, filtering grid ads on HomeDepot.com

Email: Promotional email advertising placements

In-Store: TV screens in aisles and endcaps across 100+ stores with display content and advertising

Offsite: Yahoo DSP partnership enabling reach to Home Depot shoppers across open web

Non-Endemic: Open to non-endemic advertisers including H&R Block targeting contractors for tax services

ROMO Metric: Return on Marketing Objectives metric in addition to ROAS

Pricing Model

Onsite: CPC and CPM-based pricing through Orange Access

Offsite: CPM-based through Yahoo DSP

Self-Service: Orange Access brings together onsite, offsite, and in-store for suppliers of all sizes

Performance: Minimum 2X ROAS average for network advertisers

Best Use Cases

Home Depot Orange Apron Media is ideal for home improvement, appliance, building materials, and tool brands seeking to reach DIY enthusiasts and professional contractors. The platform excels at reaching high-intent home improvement shoppers with strong conversion lift, in-store advertising during the critical decision-making moment, and non-endemic opportunities for service providers targeting homeowners and contractors. Particularly strong for brands in plumbing, electrical, flooring, paint, and outdoor living categories.

MASS AND SPECIALTY RETAIL MEDIA MARKET CONTEXT 2025
----------------------------------------

Market Growth

US retail media market projected to reach over USD 62 billion in 2025 and exceed USD 74 billion by 2026, with expectations to reach USD 129 billion by 2028. Beyond grocery and mass merchandise, specialty retailers in consumer electronics, pharmacy, and home improvement are capturing significant share by offering unique first-party data and shopper audiences not available through larger networks.

Key 2025 Trends

In-store digital advertising expanded significantly with Best Buy Store Takeovers and Home Depot screen networks. AI-powered optimization became table stakes with Target Precision Plus and Walgreens Rokt partnership. Non-endemic advertising opportunities expanded as retailers opened platforms to service providers and brands outside traditional categories. Social platform integrations deepened with Best Buy Social+ and CVS Reddit partnership creating new audience reach opportunities. Clean room and data collaboration emerged as competitive differentiators for privacy-safe measurement.

Platform Selection by Category

For mass merchandise and general CPG, Target Roundel offers AI-powered optimization with strong ROAS and extensive publisher network. For consumer electronics and technology, Best Buy Ads provides unique access to tech enthusiasts with in-store takeover capabilities. For health, wellness, and pharmacy, CVS Media Exchange and Walgreens Advertising Group offer pharmacy purchase data and health-focused audiences. For home improvement and DIY, Home Depot Orange Apron Media reaches 198 million customers with contractor and homeowner segments.

Non-Endemic Advertiser Opportunities

Mass and specialty RMNs increasingly welcome non-endemic advertisers. Best Buy Store Takeovers attract automotive and financial services brands targeting tech enthusiasts. Home Depot serves H&R Block reaching contractors for tax services. Walgreens Rokt partnership enables financial services and travel brands to reach pharmacy shoppers. These opportunities reflect retail media evolution beyond traditional shopper marketing.

Top 10 RMN Rankings - Mass and Specialty

In the top 10 US RMNs by market presence, mass and specialty retailers include: Target Roundel at USD 649 million with 25% YoY growth and USD 2 billion total value to Target, Home Depot Orange Apron Media reaching 198 million customers with 2X minimum ROAS, Best Buy Ads reaching 100+ million members with 93% transaction-to-ID linkage, CVS Media Exchange with 90 million ExtraCare members, and Walgreens Advertising Group with 101 million myWalgreens members. Combined with the five grocery RMNs, these platforms represent the top 10 US retail media networks.

Emerging Mass and Specialty RMNs

Lowes retail media network is expanding with focus on in-store audio advertising across all owned locations. eBay Ads continues developing advertising capabilities for its marketplace sellers and brands. Macys Media Network serves fashion and department store advertisers. Nordstrom Media Network targets premium fashion audiences. Ulta Beauty and Sephora are building beauty-focused retail media capabilities. These emerging networks provide category-specific targeting not available through larger generalist platforms.
