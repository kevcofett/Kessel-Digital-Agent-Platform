Inventory Pricing Benchmarks V7.0

Version 7.0 - Last Updated: December 2025

This document provides comprehensive CPM (cost per thousand impressions) benchmarks across all major digital advertising inventory types. Data reflects 2024-2025 market rates and is organized by channel, format, buying method, and targeting parameters. Use this reference for media planning, budget forecasting, and vendor negotiations.

----------------------------------------
DISPLAY ADVERTISING CPM BENCHMARKS
----------------------------------------

Standard Display by Buying Method

Display CPMs vary dramatically based on buying method, with programmatic open exchange offering the lowest rates and direct premium deals commanding significant premiums.

Open Marketplace (Programmatic): USD 0.50-2.00 for standard IAB units

Private Marketplace (PMP): USD 3.00-8.00 for curated inventory

Programmatic Guaranteed: USD 5.00-15.00 for reserved premium inventory

Direct IO (Premium Publishers): USD 8.00-25.00 depending on publisher tier

Remnant Inventory: USD 0.30-1.00 for unsold impressions

Display by Ad Format

Rich media and high-impact formats command premium pricing due to higher engagement rates and brand impact.

Standard Banner (300x250, 728x90): USD 0.50-3.00

Leaderboard (728x90): USD 1.00-4.00

Skyscraper (160x600): USD 0.80-3.50

Large Rectangle (336x280): USD 1.00-4.00

Billboard (970x250): USD 3.00-8.00

Rich Media Expandable: USD 5.00-15.00

Interstitial: USD 8.00-20.00

High Impact Takeover: USD 15.00-50.00

Sticky/Adhesion Units: USD 2.00-6.00

Display by Targeting

Adding targeting layers increases CPM due to reduced available inventory and higher value audiences.

Run of Network (No Targeting): USD 0.50-1.50

Contextual Targeting: USD 1.00-3.00 (adds 50-100% premium)

Demographic Targeting: USD 1.50-4.00

Behavioral/Interest Targeting: USD 2.00-5.00

First-Party Data Targeting: USD 3.00-8.00

Third-Party Data Targeting: USD 2.50-6.00 (plus data costs)

Retargeting/Remarketing: USD 3.00-10.00

Lookalike Audiences: USD 2.00-5.00

SEASONAL CPM VARIATIONS
----------------------------------------

Q4 Holiday Premium

Q4 CPMs increase 20-50% above annual averages due to holiday advertising demand. Black Friday through Christmas sees peak pricing.

Display Q4 Increase: 25-40% above Q1-Q3 average

Social Media Q4 Increase: 30-50% above annual average

CTV Q4 Increase: 20-35% premium

Retail Media Q4 Increase: 40-60% premium (highest demand)

Other Seasonal Factors

Back-to-School (August): 15-25% premium

Summer Travel (May-July): 10-20% premium in travel vertical

Super Bowl Week: 50-100%+ premium for sports inventory

Political Season (Election Years): 20-40% premium in swing state DMAs

Q1 January Reset: CPMs typically 15-25% below Q4 peak
