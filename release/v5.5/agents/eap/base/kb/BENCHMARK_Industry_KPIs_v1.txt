USING BENCHMARKS

Interpreting Percentiles

The 25th percentile represents below-average performance and indicates a potential improvement opportunity. The 50th percentile represents the industry median and serves as the baseline for typical performance. The 75th percentile represents above-average performance and indicates competitive advantage territory. The 90th percentile represents top performers and best-in-class performance.

Context Matters

Always consider company size, geography, and segment when applying benchmarks. Benchmarks represent industry aggregates and are not specific to individual competitors. Use benchmarks directionally rather than as absolute targets. Combine quantitative benchmarks with qualitative analysis for a complete picture.

Confidence Levels

HIGH confidence applies to well-established benchmarks with strong data sources. MEDIUM confidence applies to industry estimates with reasonable confidence levels. LOW confidence applies to limited data and should be used directionally only.
