OVERVIEW

This document contains the complete library of 32 consulting frameworks available for strategic analysis. Each framework includes purpose, methodology, typical use cases, and output components.

FRAMEWORK CATEGORIES

The frameworks are organized into five categories:
- Strategic (8 frameworks): Long-term planning and positioning
- Competitive (6 frameworks): Market and competitor analysis
- Customer (6 frameworks): Customer insights and journey
- Operations (6 frameworks): Process and efficiency improvement
- Innovation (6 frameworks): New opportunity identification

STRATEGIC FRAMEWORKS

SWOT ANALYSIS
Purpose: Evaluate Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats for strategic planning
Methodology: Identify internal strengths and weaknesses, then external opportunities and threats. Cross-reference to develop strategic options.
Use Cases: Strategic planning, competitive analysis, market entry decisions
Outputs: Strengths list, weaknesses list, opportunities list, threats list, strategic implications matrix, action priorities
Time Estimates: Quick 15 min, Standard 45 min, Comprehensive 90 min

PESTEL ANALYSIS
Purpose: Analyze macro-environmental factors affecting an organization
Methodology: Examine Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, and Legal factors systematically.
Use Cases: Market entry, strategic planning, risk assessment, industry analysis
Outputs: PESTEL factor matrix, impact assessment, trend analysis, strategic implications
Time Estimates: Quick 20 min, Standard 60 min, Comprehensive 120 min

BCG MATRIX
Purpose: Portfolio analysis to prioritize business units or products
Methodology: Plot products/units on 2x2 matrix based on market growth rate and relative market share.
Use Cases: Portfolio management, resource allocation, investment prioritization
Outputs: Product/unit classification (Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, Dogs), investment recommendations
Time Estimates: Quick 20 min, Standard 45 min, Comprehensive 90 min

ANSOFF MATRIX
Purpose: Identify growth strategies based on market and product dimensions
Methodology: Evaluate four growth options: market penetration, market development, product development, diversification.
Use Cases: Growth strategy, market expansion, product strategy, diversification decisions
Outputs: Growth strategy recommendations, risk assessment, implementation priorities
Time Estimates: Quick 15 min, Standard 40 min, Comprehensive 75 min

BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS
Purpose: Visualize and analyze business model components
Methodology: Map nine building blocks: value propositions, customer segments, channels, relationships, revenue streams, resources, activities, partnerships, cost structure.
Use Cases: Business model design, startup planning, innovation, transformation
Outputs: Completed canvas, gap analysis, improvement opportunities
Time Estimates: Quick 30 min, Standard 60 min, Comprehensive 120 min

MECE FRAMEWORK
Purpose: Structure problem decomposition ensuring completeness
Methodology: Break problems into Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive components.
Use Cases: Problem structuring, hypothesis development, analysis design
Outputs: Issue tree, hypothesis set, analysis plan
Time Estimates: Quick 15 min, Standard 30 min, Comprehensive 60 min

GAP ANALYSIS
Purpose: Identify difference between current and desired state
Methodology: Define current state, define target state, identify gaps, prioritize actions.
Use Cases: Performance improvement, transformation planning, capability assessment
Outputs: Current state assessment, target state definition, gap identification, action plan
Time Estimates: Quick 20 min, Standard 45 min, Comprehensive 90 min

SCENARIO PLANNING
Purpose: Develop strategic responses to multiple possible futures
Methodology: Identify key uncertainties, develop distinct scenarios, assess implications.
Use Cases: Strategic planning, risk management, long-term investment decisions
Outputs: Scenario descriptions, strategic implications, contingency plans
Time Estimates: Quick 30 min, Standard 90 min, Comprehensive 180 min

COMPETITIVE FRAMEWORKS

PORTERS FIVE FORCES
Purpose: Analyze industry competitive dynamics and attractiveness
Methodology: Assess five forces: supplier power, buyer power, competitive rivalry, threat of substitutes, threat of new entrants.
Use Cases: Industry analysis, competitive strategy, market entry decisions
Outputs: Five forces assessment, industry attractiveness rating, strategic recommendations
Time Estimates: Quick 20 min, Standard 60 min, Comprehensive 120 min

VRIO FRAMEWORK
Purpose: Analyze resources and capabilities for competitive advantage
Methodology: Evaluate resources on Value, Rarity, Imitability, and Organization dimensions.
Use Cases: Competitive advantage analysis, resource strategy, capability assessment
Outputs: Resource assessment, competitive implications, strategy recommendations
Time Estimates: Quick 20 min, Standard 45 min, Comprehensive 90 min

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS
Purpose: Deep assessment of specific competitors
Methodology: Analyze competitor strategy, capabilities, resources, and likely moves.
Use Cases: Competitive intelligence, strategic planning, market positioning
Outputs: Competitor profiles, capability comparison, strategic implications
Time Estimates: Quick 20 min, Standard 60 min, Comprehensive 120 min

FRAMEWORK SELECTION GUIDE

Use this quick reference to select the appropriate framework:

For Strategy Questions:
- What are our strengths and weaknesses? -> SWOT
- What external factors affect us? -> PESTEL
- Where should we invest our portfolio? -> BCG Matrix
- How should we grow? -> Ansoff Matrix
- How does our business model work? -> Business Model Canvas

For Competitive Questions:
- How attractive is this industry? -> Porters Five Forces
- What gives us competitive advantage? -> VRIO Framework
- Who are our competitors? -> Competitor Analysis
- How are competitors positioned? -> Strategic Group Mapping

For Customer Questions:
- What do customers really want? -> Jobs-to-be-Done
- What is the customer experience? -> Customer Journey Mapping
- Does our product fit customer needs? -> Value Proposition Canvas
- Which features matter most? -> Kano Model

For Operations Questions:
- Where do we add value? -> Value Chain Analysis
- Is our organization aligned? -> McKinsey 7-S
- How do we measure success? -> Balanced Scorecard
- Why is this problem occurring? -> Root Cause / Fishbone

For Innovation Questions:
- How do we create new markets? -> Blue Ocean Strategy
- How do we solve complex problems? -> Design Thinking
- How do we validate a new business? -> Lean Canvas
- How do we manage change? -> ADKAR Model
