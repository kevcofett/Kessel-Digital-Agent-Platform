OVERVIEW

This document provides industry-specific context, terminology, and strategic considerations for seven key verticals: Financial Services, Retail, CPG, QSR, Travel, Healthcare, and Technology.

FINANCIAL SERVICES

Industry Overview
Financial services encompasses banking, insurance, asset management, and fintech. The industry is characterized by heavy regulation, digital transformation, and evolving customer expectations.

Key Players by Segment

Banking Segment
The major players in banking include JPMorgan Chase, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, and Citi. These institutions represent the largest commercial banks in the United States by assets.

Insurance Segment
The major players in insurance include State Farm, Berkshire Hathaway, Allstate, and Progressive. These companies represent significant market share across property, casualty, and life insurance products.

Asset Management Segment
The major players in asset management include BlackRock, Vanguard, Fidelity, and State Street. These firms manage the largest pools of assets globally.

Fintech Segment
The major players in fintech include PayPal, Square/Block, Stripe, and Plaid. These companies are driving innovation and disruption in financial services.

Payments Segment
The major players in payments include Visa, Mastercard, and American Express. These networks process the majority of card-based transactions globally.

Strategic Priorities
- Digital transformation and mobile-first banking
- Customer experience and personalization
- Regulatory compliance (Basel III, GDPR, PSD2)
- Cybersecurity and fraud prevention
- Open banking and API ecosystems
- ESG investing and sustainable finance

Key Metrics to Track
- Net Interest Margin (NIM)
- Efficiency Ratio
- Return on Assets (ROA) / Return on Equity (ROE)
- Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Ratio
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Digital Adoption Rate

Common Challenges
- Legacy system modernization
- Fintech competition and disintermediation
- Regulatory complexity and compliance costs
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Talent acquisition in technology roles

RETAIL

Industry Overview
Retail spans brick-and-mortar stores, e-commerce, and omnichannel operations. The industry is experiencing significant disruption from digital commerce and changing consumer behaviors.

Key Players by Format

Mass Merchant Format
The major players in mass merchant retail include Walmart, Target, and Costco. These retailers offer broad product assortments at competitive prices.

Department Store Format
The major players in department stores include Macys, Nordstrom, and Kohls. These retailers focus on apparel, home goods, and branded merchandise.

Specialty Retail Format
The major players in specialty retail include Best Buy, Home Depot, and Ulta. These retailers focus on specific product categories with deep assortments.

E-commerce Format
The major players in e-commerce include Amazon, eBay, and Wayfair. These retailers operate primarily or exclusively online.

Off-Price Format
The major players in off-price retail include TJX, Ross, and Burlington. These retailers offer branded merchandise at discounted prices.

Grocery Format
The major players in grocery include Kroger, Albertsons, and Publix. These retailers focus on food and consumables.

Strategic Priorities
- Omnichannel integration and fulfillment
- E-commerce growth and profitability
- Store experience and purpose
- Inventory optimization and supply chain resilience
- Personalization and loyalty programs
- Private label development

Key Metrics to Track
- Same-Store Sales Growth
- Sales per Square Foot
- Inventory Turnover
- E-commerce Penetration
- Gross Margin
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

Common Challenges
- Amazon competitive pressure
- Store traffic decline
- Labor costs and availability
- Shrinkage and theft
- Last-mile delivery economics
- Sustainability expectations

CPG (CONSUMER PACKAGED GOODS)

Industry Overview
CPG includes food, beverages, personal care, household products, and other consumables. The industry faces pressure from private labels, changing consumer preferences, and retailer power.

Key Players by Category

Food Category
The major players in food include Nestle, PepsiCo, Kraft Heinz, and General Mills. These companies produce a wide range of packaged food products.

Beverages Category
The major players in beverages include Coca-Cola, Anheuser-Busch, and Diageo. These companies dominate soft drinks, beer, and spirits respectively.

Personal Care Category
The major players in personal care include Procter and Gamble, Unilever, LOreal, and Estee Lauder. These companies produce beauty, skincare, and hygiene products.

Household Category
The major players in household products include Procter and Gamble, Clorox, Colgate-Palmolive, and SC Johnson. These companies produce cleaning and home care products.

Snacks Category
The major players in snacks include Mondelez, Mars, Ferrero, and Hershey. These companies dominate confectionery and snack foods.

Strategic Priorities
- Brand building and premiumization
- Innovation and new product development
- Direct-to-consumer capabilities
- Trade spend optimization
- Supply chain efficiency
- Sustainability and ESG

Key Metrics to Track
- Market Share (volume and value)
- Gross Margin
- Trade Spend Efficiency
- New Product Success Rate
- Distribution (ACV)
- Brand Health Metrics

Common Challenges
- Private label competition
- Commodity cost volatility
- Retailer consolidation and power
- Changing consumer preferences (health, sustainability)
- Media fragmentation
- Innovation success rates

QSR (QUICK SERVICE RESTAURANTS)

Industry Overview
QSR includes fast food, fast casual, and coffee/snack chains. The industry is characterized by franchise models, digital ordering growth, and labor challenges.

Key Players by Segment

Burger Segment
The major players in burgers include McDonalds, Burger King, and Wendys. These chains dominate the traditional fast food burger category.

Chicken Segment
The major players in chicken include Chick-fil-A, KFC, and Popeyes. These chains have seen significant growth in recent years.

Pizza Segment
The major players in pizza include Dominos, Pizza Hut, and Papa Johns. These chains compete heavily on delivery and digital ordering.

Mexican Segment
The major players in Mexican food include Chipotle, Taco Bell, and Qdoba. Chipotle represents the fast casual approach while Taco Bell is traditional QSR.

Coffee Segment
The major players in coffee include Starbucks, Dunkin, and Dutch Bros. These chains compete on convenience, loyalty, and experience.

Sandwich Segment
The major players in sandwiches include Subway, Jersey Mikes, and Jimmy Johns. The sandwich segment has seen significant competitive shifts.

Strategic Priorities
- Digital ordering and loyalty programs
- Drive-thru and off-premise optimization
- Menu innovation and LTOs
- Franchisee health and profitability
- Labor efficiency and retention
- Real estate optimization

Key Metrics to Track
- Average Unit Volume (AUV)
- Same-Store Sales Growth
- Average Ticket Size
- Digital Sales Penetration
- Food Cost %
- Labor Cost %

Common Challenges
- Labor costs and availability
- Delivery economics and third-party fees
- Franchisee profitability pressure
- Menu complexity vs. speed of service
- Competition from fast casual
- Consumer health preferences

TRAVEL AND HOSPITALITY

Industry Overview
Travel includes airlines, hotels, cruise lines, OTAs, and travel management. The industry recovered from pandemic disruption and faces capacity constraints and technology transformation.

Key Players by Segment

Airlines Segment
The major players in airlines include Delta, United, American, and Southwest. These carriers dominate domestic US air travel.

Hotels Segment
The major players in hotels include Marriott, Hilton, IHG, and Hyatt. These chains operate thousands of properties globally across multiple brands.

Cruise Segment
The major players in cruise include Carnival, Royal Caribbean, and Norwegian. These companies operate the largest cruise fleets globally.

OTAs Segment
The major players in online travel agencies include Booking, Expedia, and Airbnb. These platforms aggregate travel inventory and facilitate bookings.

Travel Tech Segment
The major players in travel technology include Amadeus, Sabre, and Travelport. These companies provide the infrastructure for travel distribution.

Strategic Priorities
- Revenue management and pricing optimization
- Loyalty program enhancement
- Direct booking growth
- Guest experience personalization
- Sustainability and carbon reduction
- Labor management and service quality

Key Metrics to Track
- RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room)
- ADR (Average Daily Rate)
- Occupancy Rate
- GOPPAR (Gross Operating Profit per Available Room)
- Load Factor (airlines)
- Net Promoter Score

Common Challenges
- Labor shortages and costs
- OTA commission pressure
- Capacity constraints
- Sustainability expectations
- Technology modernization
- Economic sensitivity

HEALTHCARE

Industry Overview
Healthcare includes providers, payers, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, and health tech. The industry faces cost pressure, technology disruption, and regulatory complexity.

Key Players by Segment

Payers Segment
The major players in health insurance include UnitedHealth, Anthem, CVS/Aetna, and Cigna. These companies insure hundreds of millions of Americans.

Providers Segment
The major players in healthcare providers include HCA, Ascension, CommonSpirit, and Kaiser. These systems operate hospitals and care facilities.

Pharma Segment
The major players in pharmaceuticals include Pfizer, Johnson and Johnson, Merck, and AbbVie. These companies develop and manufacture drugs.

Med Devices Segment
The major players in medical devices include Medtronic, Abbott, and Boston Scientific. These companies produce medical equipment and devices.

Health Tech Segment
The major players in health technology include Epic, Cerner, Veracyte, and Teladoc. These companies provide healthcare IT and digital health solutions.

Strategic Priorities
- Value-based care transition
- Digital health and telehealth integration
- Care coordination and population health
- Revenue cycle optimization
- Clinical outcomes improvement
- Consumer experience

Key Metrics to Track
- Operating Margin
- Days Cash on Hand
- Bed Occupancy Rate
- Average Length of Stay
- Readmission Rate
- Patient Satisfaction (HCAHPS)

Common Challenges
- Reimbursement pressure
- Labor costs and burnout
- Technology integration
- Regulatory compliance
- Value-based care transition
- Health equity requirements

TECHNOLOGY

Industry Overview
Technology spans software, hardware, services, and platforms. The industry drives innovation across all sectors and is characterized by rapid change and high growth expectations.

Key Players by Segment

Cloud Segment
The major players in cloud computing include AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. These platforms provide infrastructure, platform, and software services.

Enterprise Software Segment
The major players in enterprise software include Microsoft, Salesforce, SAP, and Oracle. These companies provide business applications.

Consumer Tech Segment
The major players in consumer technology include Apple, Google, Meta, and Amazon. These companies dominate consumer devices and platforms.

Semiconductors Segment
The major players in semiconductors include NVIDIA, Intel, AMD, and TSMC. These companies design and manufacture chips.

Cybersecurity Segment
The major players in cybersecurity include CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, and Fortinet. These companies protect against cyber threats.

Strategic Priorities
- Cloud and subscription transition
- AI and machine learning integration
- Platform ecosystem expansion
- Enterprise sales effectiveness
- Product-led growth
- International expansion

Key Metrics to Track
- Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
- CAC Payback Period
- LTV:CAC Ratio
- Rule of 40
- Gross Margin

Common Challenges
- Customer acquisition costs
- Churn and retention
- Scaling sales operations
- Competition and commoditization
- Talent acquisition and retention
- Valuation pressure
