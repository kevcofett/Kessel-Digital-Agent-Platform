1.1 CORE PRINCIPLE

Measurement exists to inform decisions, not to prove success. The goal is accurate understanding of marketing impact, not favorable reporting. Honest measurement enables optimization. Biased measurement enables waste.

2.1 MARKETING MIX MODELING (MMM)

Characteristics:
- Top-down statistical analysis using aggregate data
- Measures all channels including offline
- Accounts for external factors
- Strategic timeframe (quarterly, annually)
- Privacy-safe (no user-level tracking)

Best For: Budget allocation, channel mix optimization, long-term planning

2.2 MULTI-TOUCH ATTRIBUTION (MTA)

Characteristics:
- Bottom-up analysis using user-level journey data
- Assigns credit to digital touchpoints
- Near real-time optimization
- Tactical timeframe (daily, weekly)
- Requires user-level tracking (privacy challenged)

Best For: In-flight optimization, creative testing, tactical decisions

2.3 INCREMENTALITY TESTING

Characteristics:
- Controlled experiments measuring true causal impact
- Holdout groups establish baseline
- Isolates marketing effect from organic demand
- Gold standard for proving causation

Best For: Validating channel value, major budget decisions, new channel evaluation

2.4 TRIANGULATION PRINCIPLE

No single approach provides complete truth. Triangulate:
- If MMM and MTA agree: High confidence
- If MMM and incrementality agree: Strong strategic evidence
- If MTA and incrementality disagree: Investigate attribution assumptions
- If all three disagree: Examine data quality

3.1 SINGLE-TOUCH MODELS

First-Touch Attribution:
- 100% credit to first interaction
- Best for: Awareness channel effectiveness
- Use when: Evaluating top-of-funnel campaigns

Last-Touch Attribution:
- 100% credit to final interaction
- Best for: Immediate conversion triggers
- Use when: Short cycles, direct response, limited data

3.2 MULTI-TOUCH MODELS

Linear Attribution:
- Equal credit to all touchpoints
- Simple, fair distribution
- Weakness: Ignores relative impact

Time-Decay Attribution:
- More credit to recent touchpoints
- Better for short consideration cycles
- Weakness: May undervalue awareness

Position-Based (U-Shaped):
- 40% first, 40% last, 20% middle
- Balances awareness and conversion
- Common default for complex funnels

3.3 DATA-DRIVEN ATTRIBUTION

Uses machine learning to determine credit based on actual conversion patterns.
- Requires significant conversion volume (300+/month minimum)
- Most accurate when data is sufficient
- Platform-specific (Google, Meta implementations differ)

Primary: Reach, Brand Lift, Aided Recall

4.2 CONSIDERATION OBJECTIVES

Primary: Site Visits, Time on Site, Content Engagement
Secondary: Video Views, Social Engagement, Email Signups
Validation: Retargeting pool growth, return visitor rate

4.3 CONVERSION OBJECTIVES

Primary: Conversions, Revenue, ROAS
Secondary: CVR, AOV, Cart Abandonment Rate
Validation: Incrementality tests, new customer rate

4.4 RETENTION OBJECTIVES

Primary: LTV, Repeat Purchase Rate, Churn Rate
Secondary: NPS, Customer Satisfaction, Engagement Score
Validation: Cohort analysis, retention curves

5.1 TRACKING METHODS

Client-Side (Pixel-Based):
- Standard implementation
- Subject to ad blockers and ITP
- 20-40% signal loss expected

Server-Side (CAPI):
- Direct server communication
- More reliable in privacy era
- Requires development resources

Hybrid (Recommended):
- Both client and server tracking
- Deduplication required
- Best balance of coverage and reliability

5.2 DATA QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Minimum Standards:
- Conversion pixel firing within 24 hours of conversion
- Revenue values passed accurately
- Unique transaction IDs for deduplication
- Consistent event naming across platforms
