SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

1.1 PURPOSE

This document provides comprehensive implication mapping for channel mix decisions. The agent MUST consult this document when users propose adding, removing, or significantly adjusting channel allocations. Expert advisors never simply execute channel changes without helping clients understand downstream implications across the entire media ecosystem.

1.2 SCOPE

This document covers implication chains for:
- Adding new channels to media mix
- Removing channels from media mix
- Shifting budget between channel tiers
- Changing channel role assignments
- Cross-channel interaction effects

================================================================================
SECTION 2: ADDING NEW CHANNELS
================================================================================

2.1 PAID SEARCH ADDITION IMPLICATIONS

When adding Paid Search to existing mix:
- Immediate: Captures existing demand but does not generate new demand
- Week 2-4: Attribution shifts as search captures credit from upper-funnel channels
- Week 5-8: Brand term bidding becomes competitive consideration
- Month 2-3: Non-brand performance stabilizes after learning phase
- Long-term: Becomes dependent channel requiring ongoing upper-funnel support

2.2 PAID SOCIAL ADDITION IMPLICATIONS

When adding Paid Social to existing mix:
- Immediate: Audience targeting requires 2-3 week learning phase
- Week 2-4: Creative performance patterns emerge, fatigue begins
- Week 5-8: Lookalike audiences mature, performance stabilizes
- Month 2-3: Platform algorithm optimization reaches plateau
- Long-term: Requires consistent creative refresh cycle to maintain performance

2.3 PROGRAMMATIC DISPLAY ADDITION IMPLICATIONS

When adding Programmatic Display to existing mix:
- Immediate: Broad reach at low CPM but limited direct response
- Week 2-4: Retargeting pools begin building from site visitors
- Week 5-8: Viewability and fraud monitoring critical
- Month 2-3: Cross-channel attribution effects become measurable
- Long-term: Contributes to awareness but rarely drives direct conversion

2.4 CONNECTED TV ADDITION IMPLICATIONS

When adding CTV to existing mix:
- Immediate: Premium inventory at higher CPMs than digital
- Week 2-4: Frequency management across platforms challenging
- Week 5-8: Measurement infrastructure requires development
- Month 2-3: Brand awareness lift measurable through surveys
- Long-term: Requires minimum 8-12 week flights for measurable impact

2.5 LINEAR TV ADDITION IMPLICATIONS

When adding Linear TV to existing mix:
- Immediate: Significant budget commitment with limited targeting
- Week 2-4: Production lead times require 4-8 week planning
- Week 5-8: Reach builds but frequency control limited
- Month 2-3: Brand search volume and direct traffic typically increase
- Long-term: Requires sustained investment for brand building effect

2.6 INFLUENCER ADDITION IMPLICATIONS

When adding Influencer marketing to existing mix:
- Immediate: Long lead times for creator relationship development
- Week 2-4: Content approval and production cycles variable
- Week 5-8: Engagement patterns differ from traditional channels
- Month 2-3: Attribution challenging due to indirect response path
- Long-term: Audience trust builds over sustained creator relationships

2.7 EXPERT DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS FOR CHANNEL ADDITION

When user proposes adding a channel, agent SHOULD ask:
- What objective does this channel serve that current mix does not address
- What is the minimum viable budget for this channel to be effective
- What measurement approach will validate this channel's contribution
- What internal capabilities exist to manage this channel effectively

SECTION 3: REMOVING CHANNELS
================================================================================

3.1 IMMEDIATE EFFECTS OF CHANNEL REMOVAL

When any channel is removed from mix:
- Attribution shifts to remaining channels within 1-2 weeks
- Audience touchpoint frequency decreases
- Cross-channel synergy effects diminish
- Competitor share of voice in that channel increases

3.2 UPPER-FUNNEL CHANNEL REMOVAL IMPLICATIONS

When removing awareness-focused channels:
- Week 1-4: Lower-funnel efficiency may temporarily appear to improve
- Week 5-8: Demand generation deficit begins emerging
- Month 2-3: Pipeline velocity slows as warm audiences shrink
- Month 4-6: Customer acquisition costs rise significantly
- Long-term: Brand awareness and consideration metrics decline

3.3 MID-FUNNEL CHANNEL REMOVAL IMPLICATIONS

When removing consideration-focused channels:
- Week 1-4: Upper-funnel to lower-funnel conversion rate declines
- Week 5-8: Sales cycle lengthens as nurturing touchpoints decrease
- Month 2-3: Lead quality may decline without consideration content
- Month 4-6: Lower-funnel channels work harder with less support
- Long-term: Overall marketing efficiency typically declines

3.4 LOWER-FUNNEL CHANNEL REMOVAL IMPLICATIONS

When removing conversion-focused channels:
- Week 1-4: Immediate revenue impact measurable
- Week 5-8: Upper-funnel channels appear less efficient due to attribution
- Month 2-3: Conversion rate optimization opportunities lost
- Month 4-6: Competitor captures demand you generated
- Long-term: Return on upper-funnel investment harder to demonstrate

3.5 EXPERT DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS FOR CHANNEL REMOVAL

When user proposes removing a channel, agent SHOULD ask:
- What performance data suggests this channel is underperforming
- Have you isolated channel effect from creative, audience, or timing effects
- What happens to the audience this channel was reaching
- How will you measure the impact of this removal on remaining channels

SECTION 4: CHANNEL TIER SHIFTS
================================================================================

4.1 SHIFTING FROM AWARENESS TO PERFORMANCE TIER

When reallocating from awareness to performance channels:
- Week 1-2: Performance metrics appear to improve immediately
- Week 3-6: Lead volume may increase short-term
- Week 7-12: Demand generation deficit emerges
- Month 3-6: Customer acquisition costs rise as warm audiences deplete
- Month 6+: Brand awareness and consideration metrics decline measurably

4.2 SHIFTING FROM PERFORMANCE TO AWARENESS TIER

When reallocating from performance to awareness channels:
- Week 1-2: Performance metrics decline immediately
- Week 3-6: Lead volume decreases, sales pressure increases
- Week 7-12: Brand awareness begins improving
- Month 3-6: Consideration and intent metrics improve
- Month 6+: Performance efficiency typically improves as brand strengthens

4.3 BALANCED TIER ADJUSTMENT IMPLICATIONS

When adjusting both tiers proportionally:
- Week 1-2: Minimal immediate disruption
- Week 3-6: Cross-channel synergy effects adjust gradually
- Week 7-12: New equilibrium establishes
- Month 3-6: Performance patterns reflect new allocation
- Month 6+: Baseline reestablished for future optimization

4.4 EXPERT DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS FOR TIER SHIFTS

When user proposes tier reallocation, agent SHOULD ask:
- What business objective is driving this shift
- What timeline exists for evaluating the tier shift success
- Are stakeholders aligned on expected short-term performance changes
- What monitoring will trigger reversal if results are negative

================================================================================
SECTION 5: CHANNEL ROLE CHANGES
================================================================================

5.1 CHANGING CHANNEL FROM PROSPECTING TO RETARGETING

When shifting channel focus to retargeting:
- Immediate: Audience pools must be sufficient to support spend
- Week 2-4: Efficiency metrics typically improve
- Week 5-8: Pool depletion risk if prospecting insufficient
- Month 2-3: Incrementality declines as you reach already-interested users
- Long-term: Requires prospecting support to maintain pool health

5.2 CHANGING CHANNEL FROM RETARGETING TO PROSPECTING

When shifting channel focus to prospecting:
- Immediate: Efficiency metrics typically decline
- Week 2-4: Learning phase as algorithm finds new audiences
- Week 5-8: Reach expands but conversion rate decreases
- Month 2-3: New audience segments discovered
- Long-term: Builds future retargeting pools and brand awareness

5.3 CHANGING CHANNEL FROM BRAND TO PERFORMANCE FOCUS

When shifting channel from brand to performance creative:
- Immediate: Conversion metrics typically improve
- Week 2-4: Brand awareness contribution declines
- Week 5-8: Audience response patterns shift
- Month 2-3: Channel becomes optimization-dependent
- Long-term: May reduce overall marketing effectiveness if brand support lost

5.4 EXPERT DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS FOR ROLE CHANGES

When user proposes channel role change, agent SHOULD ask:
- What performance signal suggests the current role is not working
- Are audience pools sufficient to support the new role
- How will this role change affect other channels in the mix
- What metrics will validate the role change success

SECTION 6: CROSS-CHANNEL INTERACTION EFFECTS
================================================================================

6.1 POSITIVE INTERACTION EFFECTS

Channels that enhance each other when combined:
- TV plus Search: TV drives search volume, search captures demand
- Social plus Display: Social engagement builds retargeting pools for display
- Email plus Paid Media: Email nurtures paid media visitors
- Content plus Search: Content builds organic authority, enhances paid quality score
- Influencer plus Social: Creator content performs in paid social

6.2 NEGATIVE INTERACTION EFFECTS

Channels that may conflict when combined:
- Overlapping retargeting across channels: Frequency overload
- Brand search cannibalization: Paid search capturing organic traffic
- Affiliate plus direct: Attribution conflicts and margin erosion
- Multiple DSPs in programmatic: Bidding against yourself
- Overlapping influencer audiences: Diminishing incremental reach

6.3 ATTRIBUTION INTERACTION EFFECTS

How channel changes affect attribution:
- Adding channels typically increases path length
- Removing channels concentrates attribution in remaining channels
- Last-click attribution overstates lower-funnel contribution
- Multi-touch attribution reveals true channel interaction value
- Incrementality testing isolates true channel contribution

6.4 EXPERT DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS FOR INTERACTION EFFECTS

When evaluating channel interactions, agent SHOULD ask:
- What is the current average path length to conversion
- How much audience overlap exists between channels
- Are you seeing frequency issues across channel combinations
- What attribution model are you using to evaluate channel contribution

SECTION 7: CHANNEL-SPECIFIC IMPLICATION DETAILS
================================================================================

7.1 PAID SEARCH IMPLICATIONS

Adding Paid Search:
- Captures existing demand generated by other channels
- Attribution will shift credit from upper-funnel channels
- Brand bidding decisions required within 30-60 days
- Requires ongoing optimization to maintain efficiency

Removing Paid Search:
- Competitors capture demand you generated elsewhere
- Conversion attribution shifts to other lower-funnel channels
- Brand search still occurs but without capture
- Lost ability to control brand messaging in search

7.2 PAID SOCIAL IMPLICATIONS

Adding Paid Social:
- 2-3 week learning phase before performance stabilizes
- Creative fatigue cycles require ongoing content production
- Audience targeting matures over 4-8 weeks
- Platform dependency increases for first-party data

Removing Paid Social:
- Lose mid-funnel engagement and consideration touchpoints
- Retargeting pool building from social engagement stops
- Competitor presence increases in social feeds
- Influencer amplification opportunities lost

7.3 PROGRAMMATIC DISPLAY IMPLICATIONS

Adding Programmatic Display:
- Broad reach at low cost but limited direct response
- Viewability and fraud monitoring essential
- Retargeting pools build over 4-6 weeks
- Brand safety configuration required

Removing Programmatic Display:
- Awareness reach decreases significantly
- Retargeting pools for other channels shrink
- Competitor display presence increases
- Cross-device tracking capabilities reduced

7.4 CONNECTED TV IMPLICATIONS

Adding CTV:
- Premium reach with limited targeting compared to digital
- Frequency management across CTV platforms challenging
- Minimum 8-12 week flights for measurable brand impact
- Measurement requires additional infrastructure

Removing CTV:
- Premium audience reach decreases
- Brand awareness contribution declines
- Competitor CTV presence increases
- Full-funnel attribution path shortens

7.5 LINEAR TV IMPLICATIONS

Adding Linear TV:
- Significant budget commitment with limited targeting
- 4-8 week production and planning lead times
- Reach builds but frequency control limited
- Brand search volume typically increases 2-4 weeks after flight

Removing Linear TV:
- Broad awareness reach declines significantly
- Brand search volume typically decreases
- Competitor TV presence increases
- Requires 8-12 weeks to assess full impact

SECTION 8: IMPLICATION CHAIN EXAMPLES
================================================================================

8.1 EXAMPLE: ADDING CTV TO EXISTING MIX

User scenario: We want to add 75K in CTV to our Q2 plan.

Agent MUST communicate this implication chain:
- Week 1-2: CTV enters at premium CPMs, frequency management across platforms critical
- Week 3-4: Cross-device matching begins building attribution paths
- Week 5-8: Measurement infrastructure development needed for accurate reporting
- Month 2-3: Brand awareness lift becomes measurable through survey methodology
- Month 3-4: Cross-channel synergy with search and social becomes apparent

Agent SHOULD recommend:
- Plan minimum 8-12 week flight for measurable impact
- Establish measurement baseline before launch
- Coordinate creative messaging with existing channels
- Set realistic expectations for attribution complexity

8.2 EXAMPLE: REMOVING PROGRAMMATIC DISPLAY

User scenario: Display performance is poor, we want to cut it entirely.

Agent MUST communicate this implication chain:
- Week 1-2: Attribution shifts to remaining channels, especially search and social
- Week 3-4: Retargeting pools for other channels begin shrinking
- Week 5-8: Awareness reach decreases, upper-funnel coverage gaps emerge
- Month 2-3: Other channels may show efficiency decline as warm audience shrinks
- Month 3-4: Competitor display presence increases in your category

Agent SHOULD recommend:
- Confirm display underperformance is not creative or targeting issue
- Consider reducing rather than eliminating to preserve retargeting capability
- Monitor other channel efficiency for delayed effects
- Set triggers for restoring display if efficiency decline appears

8.3 EXAMPLE: SHIFTING FROM SOCIAL TO SEARCH

User scenario: Move 50K from Paid Social to Paid Search.

Agent MUST communicate this implication chain:
- Week 1-2: Social reach and engagement decline, search captures more demand
- Week 3-4: Social retargeting pools shrink, search efficiency may improve short-term
- Week 5-8: Demand generation from social declines, search may see volume decrease
- Month 2-3: Overall lead volume typically declines as demand generation shrinks
- Month 3-4: Efficiency gains in search plateau as warm audience depletes

Agent SHOULD recommend:
- Confirm social underperformance is not creative or audience issue
- Understand that search captures demand but social generates it
- Phase the shift to monitor demand generation impact
- Set realistic expectations for medium-term efficiency changes

SECTION 10: AGENT RESPONSE PATTERNS
================================================================================

10.1 WHEN USER PROPOSES ADDING A CHANNEL

Agent MUST:
- Acknowledge the opportunity
- Articulate learning phase and timeline expectations
- Explain cross-channel interaction effects
- Recommend minimum viable budget and flight length

Example response pattern:
Adding [channel] to your mix creates opportunity for [objective]. This channel typically requires a 2-4 week learning phase before performance stabilizes. Cross-channel effects include [synergy opportunities]. For meaningful impact, recommend minimum [budget] over [flight length]. What measurement approach will validate this channel's contribution?

10.2 WHEN USER PROPOSES REMOVING A CHANNEL

Agent MUST:
- Acknowledge the rationale
- Articulate immediate and delayed implications
- Explain cross-channel attribution shifts
- Recommend reduction versus elimination if appropriate

Example response pattern:
Removing [channel] will produce immediate and delayed effects. Attribution will shift to remaining channels within 1-2 weeks. By weeks 5-8, [specific impact] typically emerges. Other channels may see [efficiency changes] as [explanation]. Before complete removal, confirm [channel] underperformance is not [alternative cause]. Consider reducing to [amount] to preserve [capability] while testing impact.

10.3 WHEN USER PROPOSES CHANNEL REALLOCATION

Agent MUST:
- Acknowledge the strategy
- Articulate source and destination channel implications
- Explain cross-channel interaction effects
- Recommend phased approach with monitoring

Example response pattern:
Shifting from [source] to [destination] will affect both channels. [Source] will experience [immediate effects] while [destination] enters learning phase. By weeks 5-8, [cross-channel effects] typically emerge. Recommend phasing over 2-4 weeks to limit disruption and enable course correction. What performance signal is driving this reallocation?
