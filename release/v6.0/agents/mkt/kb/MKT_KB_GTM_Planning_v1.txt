GO-TO-MARKET PLANNING

PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document provides comprehensive guidance for developing go-to-market strategies that successfully launch products services or market entries. Effective GTM planning coordinates all elements required to reach target customers and achieve commercial objectives.

GTM PLANNING FUNDAMENTALS

DEFINITION AND SCOPE

Go-to-Market Strategy
- Coordinated plan for reaching target customers
- Framework for commercial launch execution
- Integration of product pricing distribution and promotion
- Roadmap from development to market success

GTM Versus Marketing Strategy
- GTM focuses on launch and market entry
- Marketing strategy covers ongoing market presence
- GTM has defined timeline and milestones
- Marketing strategy is continuous and evolving

GTM PLANNING PROCESS

PHASE ONE MARKET ANALYSIS

Market Opportunity Assessment
- Total addressable market size and growth
- Serviceable addressable market within reach
- Serviceable obtainable market realistic target
- Market timing and readiness indicators

Competitive Landscape
- Direct competitors and their positions
- Indirect competitors and substitutes
- Competitive advantages and vulnerabilities
- Expected competitive response to entry

Customer Analysis
- Target segment identification and sizing
- Customer needs and pain points
- Buying process and decision criteria
- Willingness to pay and value perception

- Segment prioritization rationale

Pricing Strategy
- Pricing model selection
- Price point determination
- Discount and promotion structure
- Competitive price positioning

PRICING STRATEGY

PRICING MODELS

Subscription Pricing
- Recurring revenue model
- Predictable cash flow
- Customer lifetime value focus
- Retention as key metric

Transaction Pricing
- Per-purchase revenue model
- Usage-based economics
- Volume as key driver
- Repeat purchase focus

Freemium Pricing
- Free tier for acquisition
- Premium tier for monetization
- Conversion rate optimization
- Network effect potential

PRICE SETTING METHODS

Cost-Plus Pricing
- Production cost calculation
- Margin target application
- Simple to implement
- Ignores market dynamics

Value-Based Pricing
- Customer value quantification
- Willingness to pay research
- Captures value created
- Requires deep understanding

Competitive Pricing
- Market price benchmarking
- Position relative to competitors
- Market share considerations
- Assumes rational market

PRICE OPTIMIZATION

Testing Approaches
- A/B testing at different prices
- Conjoint analysis for preference
- Van Westendorp price sensitivity
- Gabor-Granger direct questioning

Dynamic Considerations
- Launch pricing versus steady state
- Promotional pricing strategy
- Price changes over lifecycle
- Segment-specific pricing
