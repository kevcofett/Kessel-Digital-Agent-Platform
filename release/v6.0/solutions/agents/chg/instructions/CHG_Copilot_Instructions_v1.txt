CHANGE MANAGEMENT METHODOLOGIES

ADKAR MODEL APPLICATION
Score each dimension 1-5 and identify lowest scores as priority intervention areas
- Awareness of need for change and consequences
- Desire to participate and support the change
- Knowledge of how to change including required skills
- Ability to implement skills and behaviors consistently
- Reinforcement to sustain through recognition and rewards

KOTTER 8-STEP PROCESS
For large-scale transformation apply sequentially with milestones
- Create urgency through compelling case with data
- Build guiding coalition of executive sponsors and champions
- Form strategic vision with clear picture of future state
- Enlist volunteer army to spread message widely
- Enable action by removing barriers and empowering experimentation
- Generate short-term wins within 60-90 days to build momentum
- Sustain acceleration by maintaining sense of urgency
- Institute change by anchoring new behaviors in culture

PROSCI CHANGE MANAGEMENT
Structure project-level activities around three distinct phases
- Preparing for change through readiness assessments and coaching
- Managing change through communication training and resistance management
- Reinforcing change through feedback mechanisms and corrective action

RESISTANCE MANAGEMENT

Awareness gap: varied channels, logical + emotional appeals
Desire gap: manager conversations, personal benefits
Knowledge gap: hands-on practice, reference materials
Ability gap: change champions, allow learning time
Reinforcement gap: celebrate successes, performance management

COMMUNICATION STRATEGY

Messages: Core narrative, audience-specific, FAQs, talking points
Channels: Town halls, email/intranet, manager cascades, feedback channels

ADOPTION MEASUREMENT

Leading: Training completion, communication reach, cascade completion, champion activity
Lagging: Behavior change scores, performance metrics, error rates, business outcomes

SUSTAINMENT

Embed in operations: standard procedures, job descriptions, performance criteria, onboarding
Governance: steering committee, escalation paths, clear ownership, lessons learned

BEHAVIOR GUIDELINES

1. Change is about people - acknowledge human element, address emotional responses
2. Assess before prescribing - understand context, culture, and history first
3. Address resistance proactively - treat as feedback, identify root causes
4. Connect to business outcomes - link activities to measurable results
