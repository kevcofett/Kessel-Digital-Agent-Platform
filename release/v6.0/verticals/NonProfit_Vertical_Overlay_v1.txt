SECTION 2 - KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS
================================================================================

FUNDRAISING METRICS

DONATIONS
Total funds raised from individuals.
Track by source, amount, frequency.

COST PER DONATION (CPD)
Marketing cost to generate one donation.
CPD = Marketing_Spend / Number_of_Donations

COST PER DOLLAR RAISED (CPDR)
Marketing cost relative to revenue.
CPDR = Marketing_Spend / Total_Donations_Raised

TYPICAL CPDR RANGES
- Acquisition: $0.75-1.50 per dollar raised
- Retention: $0.10-0.30 per dollar raised
- Blended: $0.25-0.50 per dollar raised

AVERAGE GIFT
Mean donation amount.
Track by source and campaign.

DONOR METRICS

DONOR COUNT
Total unique donors.
Growth rate indicates health.

NEW DONOR ACQUISITION
First-time donors acquired.
Critical for growth.

DONOR RETENTION RATE
Percentage of donors giving again.
Industry average: 40-50%.
Retention_Rate = Returning_Donors / Prior_Year_Donors x 100

DONOR UPGRADE RATE
Percentage increasing gift amount.
Upgrade strategies important.

RECURRING DONORS
Donors on automatic giving programs.
High value, stable revenue.
Monthly giving programs growing.

DONOR LIFETIME VALUE

DONOR LTV CALCULATION
LTV = Average_Annual_Gift x Average_Years_as_Donor

SIMPLIFIED LTV
LTV = Average_Gift x (1 / (1 - Retention_Rate))

EXAMPLE
- Average annual gift: $150
- Retention rate: 45%
- LTV = $150 x (1 / 0.55) = $273

LTV TO CAC RATIO
Acquisition cost should be fraction of LTV.
Target: Recover CAC within 1-2 years.

ADVOCACY METRICS

PETITION SIGNATURES
Supporters signing petitions.
Lead generation equivalent.

EMAIL SIGN-UPS
Growing supporter list.
Foundation for future engagement.

ACTIONS TAKEN
Calls, emails, social actions.
Advocacy engagement measure.

VOLUNTEER SIGN-UPS
Supporter to volunteer conversion.
Deep engagement indicator.

ATTRIBUTION APPROACH

MULTI-TOUCH ATTRIBUTION
Donors often have multiple touchpoints before giving.

ATTRIBUTION WINDOWS
- Click-through: 30-60 days (or longer)
- View-through: 7-14 days
- Consider longer windows for cultivation

OFFLINE ATTRIBUTION
- Direct mail response tracking
- Phone donations
- Event giving
- Major gifts

INCREMENTALITY
Test marketing impact on donation behavior.
Holdout testing for key campaigns.

EFFICIENCY METRICS

COST PER DOLLAR RAISED (CPDR)
CPDR = Marketing_Spend / Total_Raised

BENCHMARKS
- Acquisition: $0.75-1.50
- Retention: $0.10-0.30
- Blended: $0.25-0.50

RETURN ON FUNDRAISING INVESTMENT
ROFI = (Total_Raised - Fundraising_Cost) / Fundraising_Cost

EXAMPLE
- Raised: $1,000,000
- Spent: $300,000
- ROFI = ($1,000,000 - $300,000) / $300,000 = 2.33

This means $2.33 net raised for every $1 spent.

SECTION 9 - AGENT GUIDANCE
================================================================================

PLANNING RECOMMENDATIONS

BUDGET ALLOCATION FRAMEWORK
- Year-end campaigns: 30-40%
- Always-on acquisition: 25-35%
- Retention programs: 20-25%
- Emergency reserve: 5-10%

CHANNEL PRIORITIZATION
1. Email (foundational, highest ROI)
2. Paid social (primary acquisition)
3. Google Grants (free search)
4. Display/retargeting (nurture)
5. Video (awareness)

EFFICIENCY TARGETS

ACQUISITION TARGETS
- Cost per new donor: $50-150
- CPDR for acquisition: Under $1.50
- First gift to second gift: 25%+

RETENTION TARGETS
- Donor retention: 45%+
- Recurring conversion: 5-10%
- CPDR for retention: Under $0.25

OPTIMIZATION PRIORITIES

ACQUISITION OPTIMIZATION
- Test emotional appeals
- Optimize landing pages
- Refine targeting
- Test gift amount suggestions

RETENTION OPTIMIZATION
- Improve stewardship
- Personalize communications
- Upgrade systematically
- Reactivate lapsed donors

WARNING SIGNS
- Rising acquisition costs
- Declining retention rates
- Average gift declining
- Response rates falling
- Unsubscribe rates increasing

SEASONAL CONSIDERATIONS
- Plan year-end early (September)
- Giving Tuesday preparation
- Tax deadline opportunity
- Cause-specific awareness periods
