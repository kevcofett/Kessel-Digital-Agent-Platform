SECTION 1 - CONTEXTUAL TARGETING FUNDAMENTALS
================================================================================

CONTEXTUAL TARGETING DEFINITION

Contextual targeting places ads based on the content of the page or environment where the ad appears, rather than on user data or behavioral history.

CONTEXTUAL VS BEHAVIORAL

CONTEXTUAL TARGETING
- Based on page content
- No user data required
- Privacy-friendly
- Real-time analysis
- Environment-focused

BEHAVIORAL TARGETING
- Based on user history
- Requires user data
- Privacy concerns
- Historical patterns
- User-focused

WHY CONTEXTUAL MATTERS NOW
- Cookie deprecation
- Privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA)
- Mobile identifier restrictions
- Consumer privacy expectations
- Brand safety alignment

CONTEXTUAL ADVANTAGES
- Privacy compliant by design
- No consent requirements for targeting
- Works in cookieless environments
- Aligns ads with relevant content
- Brand safety integration

SECTION 2 - CONTEXTUAL TARGETING METHODS
================================================================================

KEYWORD TARGETING

DEFINITION
Target pages containing specific keywords.

HOW IT WORKS
1. Define target keywords
2. System scans page content
3. Match keywords to content
4. Serve ads on matching pages

KEYWORD STRATEGIES
- Broad keywords for reach
- Specific keywords for relevance
- Negative keywords for exclusions
- Keyword combinations

LIMITATIONS
- Context of keyword missed
- Homonyms cause issues (apple fruit vs Apple company)
- Limited semantic understanding
- Can be too literal

CATEGORY TARGETING

DEFINITION
Target pages classified into content categories.

CATEGORY TAXONOMIES
- IAB Content Taxonomy
- Publisher-specific categories
- Custom category definitions
- Hierarchical classification

HOW IT WORKS
1. Pages classified by category
2. Advertisers select categories
3. Ads served on matching pages
4. Exclusion categories available

EXAMPLE CATEGORIES
- Business/Finance
- Sports/Football
- Technology/Mobile
- Entertainment/Movies
- Health/Fitness

ADVANTAGES
- Broader than keywords
- Standard taxonomies available
- Easier to scale
- Less granular setup

SEMANTIC CONTEXTUAL

DEFINITION
Advanced contextual using natural language understanding to analyze page meaning.

HOW IT WORKS
1. Analyze full page content
2. Understand semantic meaning
3. Extract topics and sentiment
4. Score relevance to ad content

CAPABILITIES
- Understanding context, not just keywords
- Sentiment analysis
- Entity recognition
- Topic modeling
- Brand suitability scoring

ADVANTAGES
- More accurate relevance
- Handles nuance and context
- Better brand safety
- Reduces false positives

VISUAL CONTEXTUAL

DEFINITION
Analyze images and video content for contextual signals.

HOW IT WORKS
1. Computer vision analyzes images/video
2. Identify objects, scenes, emotions
3. Classify visual content
4. Match ads to visual context

APPLICATIONS
- Video advertising
- Image-heavy content
- User-generated content
- Visual brand safety

SECTION 4 - BRAND SAFETY AND SUITABILITY
================================================================================

BRAND SAFETY DEFINITION

Brand safety ensures ads do not appear alongside content that could harm brand reputation.

GARM FRAMEWORK

GLOBAL ALLIANCE FOR RESPONSIBLE MEDIA
Industry standard for brand safety categories.

GARM CATEGORIES
- Adult and explicit sexual content
- Arms and ammunition
- Crime and harmful acts
- Death, injury, military conflict
- Online piracy
- Hate speech and discrimination
- Obscenity and profanity
- Illegal drugs, tobacco, alcohol
- Spam or harmful content
- Terrorism
- Debated sensitive social issues

BRAND SUITABILITY DEFINITION

Brand suitability goes beyond safety to align with brand values and campaign context.

SUITABILITY CONSIDERATIONS
- Brand tone and voice
- Campaign message alignment
- Audience appropriateness
- Competitive context
- Content quality

SUITABILITY SPECTRUM
- Brand unsafe: Always avoid
- Low suitability: Avoid for most brands
- Medium suitability: Context dependent
- High suitability: Generally appropriate

CONTEXTUAL FOR BRAND SAFETY

PRE-BID BLOCKING
Block before bidding on unsafe inventory.
Most efficient approach.

POST-BID VERIFICATION
Verify after ad served.
Identifies issues, does not prevent.

CUSTOM CONTROLS
Define brand-specific rules.
Beyond standard categories.

SECTION 5 - CONTEXTUAL STRATEGY DEVELOPMENT
================================================================================

STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

ALIGNMENT APPROACH
Match ad content to page content for relevance.
Example: Running shoes ad on fitness content.

INTEREST EXPANSION
Target content interests of target audience.
Example: Luxury car ad on business content.

MOMENT TARGETING
Target content related to consumption moments.
Example: Snack ad on entertainment content.

COMPETITOR ADJACENCY
Target content where competitors appear.
Example: Your brand on competitor review content.

CONTEXTUAL SEGMENT DEVELOPMENT

CUSTOM SEGMENT CREATION
1. Define campaign objectives
2. Identify relevant content themes
3. Develop keyword and category lists
4. Test and refine targeting
5. Measure and optimize

SEGMENT COMPONENTS
- Include keywords and categories
- Exclude irrelevant or unsafe content
- Set relevance thresholds
- Apply brand safety filters

TESTING APPROACH
- A/B test contextual vs behavioral
- Test narrow vs broad targeting
- Measure performance differences
- Optimize based on results

BALANCING REACH AND RELEVANCE

NARROW TARGETING
- High relevance
- Lower reach
- Higher CPMs
- Stronger performance

BROAD TARGETING
- Lower relevance
- Higher reach
- Lower CPMs
- Efficiency at scale

OPTIMIZATION APPROACH
Start broad, identify performing contexts, narrow targeting.

SECTION 6 - PERFORMANCE MEASUREMENT
================================================================================

CONTEXTUAL PERFORMANCE METRICS

REACH METRICS
- Impressions by context
- Unique reach by context
- Available inventory

ENGAGEMENT METRICS
- CTR by context category
- Viewability by context
- Video completion by context

OUTCOME METRICS
- Conversion rate by context
- CPA by context
- ROAS by context

CONTEXTUAL VS BEHAVIORAL COMPARISON

TESTING FRAMEWORK
- Split traffic between approaches
- Same creative and offers
- Measure outcomes
- Statistical significance

TYPICAL FINDINGS
- Contextual often matches or exceeds behavioral
- Results vary by category and creative
- Contextual wins on brand metrics
- Behavioral may win on retargeting

OPTIMIZATION APPROACH

CONTEXT PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
1. Measure performance by context segment
2. Identify top performing contexts
3. Increase investment in winners
4. Test new context hypotheses

NEGATIVE CONTEXTS
Identify contexts that underperform:
- High CPM, low conversion
- Brand safety concerns
- Irrelevant traffic
- Add to exclusion lists

SECTION 7 - CONTEXTUAL INTEGRATION
================================================================================

CONTEXTUAL PLUS AUDIENCE

HYBRID TARGETING
Combine contextual with audience data for precision.

APPROACHES
- Audience targeted, context filtered
- Context targeted, audience filtered
- Contextual for prospecting, audience for retargeting

BENEFITS
- Best of both approaches
- Privacy-friendly base
- Audience enhancement where available

CONTEXTUAL PLUS ATTENTION

ATTENTION-WEIGHTED CONTEXTUAL
Prioritize contexts with higher attention.

INTEGRATION
- Score contexts by attention potential
- Bid higher for high-attention contexts
- Optimize for attention-adjusted outcomes

SECTION 8 - AGENT APPLICATION GUIDANCE
================================================================================

WHEN TO RECOMMEND CONTEXTUAL

STRONG FIT
- Privacy-first requirements
- Brand safety priority
- Cookieless environments
- Relevant content alignment
- New audience prospecting

CONTEXTUAL-FIRST SCENARIOS
- Regulated industries
- Sensitive brands
- European markets (GDPR)
- Apple ecosystem (ATT)
- Cookieless browsers

CONTEXTUAL STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS

FOR AWARENESS CAMPAIGNS
- Broad category targeting
- Reach-focused contexts
- Content affinity alignment
- Brand safety filters

FOR CONSIDERATION CAMPAIGNS
- Interest-aligned contexts
- Competitive adjacency
- Content quality focus
- Engagement optimization

FOR CONVERSION CAMPAIGNS
- High-intent contexts
- Purchase-related content
- Product category alignment
- Performance optimization

TARGETING RECOMMENDATIONS

STARTING POINT
- Begin with broad categories
- Layer brand safety
- Monitor performance
- Refine based on results

OPTIMIZATION PATH
1. Analyze context performance
2. Identify top performers
3. Create custom segments
4. Exclude underperformers
5. Test new hypotheses

WARNING SIGNS
- Very low reach: Targeting too narrow
- High CPM without performance: Wrong contexts
- Brand safety incidents: Review exclusions
- Performance variance: Context quality inconsistent

CONTEXTUAL INVESTMENT GUIDANCE

BUDGET ALLOCATION
- Include contextual in all plans
- Increase share as cookies decline
- Test against behavioral baseline
- Optimize based on results

VENDOR SELECTION
- Evaluate targeting sophistication
- Check coverage and reach
- Review brand safety capabilities
- Consider integration complexity
