SECTION 1 - GOOGLE ADS CAMPAIGN TYPES
================================================================================

SEARCH CAMPAIGNS

PURPOSE AND ROLE
Search campaigns capture high-intent users actively searching for products or services. Primary lower-funnel channel for demand capture.

CAMPAIGN SUBTYPES

STANDARD SEARCH
- Manual keyword targeting
- Full control over match types
- Ad group structure determines relevance
- Best for: Established accounts with known keywords

DYNAMIC SEARCH ADS (DSA)
- Automatically targets based on website content
- Generates headlines dynamically
- Captures long-tail queries
- Best for: Large catalogs, gap filling

CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE BEST PRACTICES
- Single Theme Ad Groups (STAGs) for control
- 3-5 responsive search ads per ad group
- Pin critical messaging elements
- Include all relevant extensions

MATCH TYPE STRATEGY

BROAD MATCH
- Widest reach, relies on Smart Bidding signals
- Requires sufficient conversion data
- Best with Target CPA or Target ROAS bidding
- Monitor search terms report weekly

PHRASE MATCH
- Captures queries with keyword meaning
- Balance of reach and control
- Include negatives proactively

EXACT MATCH
- Tightest targeting, highest relevance
- Lower volume, higher efficiency
- Use for brand terms and top performers

PERFORMANCE MAX CAMPAIGNS

PURPOSE AND ROLE
Performance Max (PMax) uses AI to optimize across all Google inventory from a single campaign. Consolidates Search, Display, YouTube, Discover, Gmail, and Maps.

ASSET GROUP STRUCTURE

ASSET REQUIREMENTS
- Headlines: 3-5 short, 1-2 long
- Descriptions: 2-4 variations
- Images: Multiple aspect ratios (1.91:1, 1:1, 4:5)
- Videos: 10+ seconds recommended
- Logos: Square and landscape

ASSET GROUP STRATEGY
- One asset group per product category or audience theme
- Do not mix significantly different products
- Separate asset groups for different value propositions

AUDIENCE SIGNALS

PURPOSE
Guide the algorithm toward likely converters. Not hard targeting.

SIGNAL TYPES
- Custom segments (search terms, URLs, apps)
- Your data (customer lists, website visitors)
- Interests and demographics
- Life events

SIGNAL BEST PRACTICES
- Layer multiple signal types
- Include competitor URLs in custom segments
- Upload high-value customer lists
- Refresh signals quarterly

PMAX OPTIMIZATION LEVERS
- Asset performance ratings (replace Low performers)
- URL expansion settings (enable or restrict)
- Final URL expansion (test both settings)
- Search themes (new feature for search guidance)
- Brand exclusions (protect brand traffic)

DEMAND GEN CAMPAIGNS

PURPOSE AND ROLE
Mid-funnel campaigns for visual storytelling across YouTube, Discover, and Gmail. Replaces Discovery campaigns.

CREATIVE REQUIREMENTS
- Video: Vertical (9:16), Square (1:1), Horizontal (16:9)
- Image: Multiple aspect ratios required
- Carousel: Up to 10 cards supported
- Product feeds: Can connect for dynamic creative

AUDIENCE TARGETING
- Lookalike segments (seed from customer lists)
- Custom segments for interest targeting
- Remarketing audiences
- Optimized targeting expands beyond selections

BIDDING OPTIONS
- Maximize conversions
- Maximize conversion value
- Target CPA (requires history)
- Target ROAS (requires history)

SECTION 2 - SMART BIDDING STRATEGIES
================================================================================

BIDDING STRATEGY SELECTION

TARGET CPA (COST PER ACQUISITION)
- Goal: Achieve target cost per conversion
- Requirement: 30+ conversions in last 30 days recommended
- Best for: Lead generation, stable conversion values

TARGET ROAS (RETURN ON AD SPEND)
- Goal: Achieve target return on ad spend
- Requirement: 50+ conversions in last 30 days recommended
- Best for: Ecommerce, variable conversion values
- Note: Platform uses revenue/spend formula, not correct ROAS

MAXIMIZE CONVERSIONS
- Goal: Get most conversions within budget
- No target set, spends full budget
- Best for: New campaigns building history
- Risk: May acquire low-quality conversions

MAXIMIZE CONVERSION VALUE
- Goal: Get most revenue within budget
- No target set, spends full budget
- Best for: Building toward Target ROAS

BIDDING THRESHOLDS AND RAMP-UP

LEARNING PERIOD
- Duration: Typically 1-2 weeks
- Performance: May fluctuate during learning
- Changes: Significant edits restart learning

RAMP-UP STRATEGY
- Start with Maximize Conversions
- Accumulate 30-50 conversions
- Set conservative Target CPA (20% above observed)
- Gradually tighten targets (5-10% per 2 weeks)
- Monitor for volume vs efficiency trade-off

TARGET SETTING GUIDANCE

TARGET CPA CALCULATION
Start with: Historical_CPA x 1.15 to 1.25

TARGET ROAS CALCULATION
Start with: Historical_ROAS x 0.80 to 0.90
Accounting for correct ROAS: (Revenue - Spend) / Spend

ADJUSTMENT CADENCE
- Allow 2 weeks between target changes
- Adjust by no more than 15-20% at once
- Monitor impression share for headroom

PORTFOLIO BIDDING

PORTFOLIO BENEFITS
- Shared learning across campaigns
- Single target for related campaigns
- Easier management at scale

PORTFOLIO STRUCTURE
- Group campaigns with similar goals
- Keep separate by product margin tiers
- Avoid mixing brand and non-brand

SECTION 3 - AUDIENCE STRATEGIES
================================================================================

FIRST-PARTY AUDIENCES

CUSTOMER MATCH
- Upload hashed email lists
- Match rates: 30-60% typical
- Use for: Exclusions, remarketing, similar audiences
- Refresh: Monthly minimum

WEBSITE REMARKETING
- All visitors (broad, for exclusions)
- Product viewers (consideration stage)
- Cart abandoners (high intent)
- Converters (for exclusion or cross-sell)

REMARKETING WINDOW GUIDANCE
- Impulse products: 7-14 days
- Considered purchases: 30-60 days
- High-ticket items: 90-180 days

GOOGLE AUDIENCES

IN-MARKET AUDIENCES
- Users actively researching category
- Strong intent signal
- Good for prospecting expansion

AFFINITY AUDIENCES
- Long-term interests and habits
- Broader reach, lower intent
- Best for awareness goals

CUSTOM SEGMENTS

CUSTOM INTENT (KEYWORDS)
- Target users who searched specific terms on Google
- Reaches users across Display and YouTube
- Combine branded and category terms

CUSTOM INTENT (URLS)
- Target users who browse specific websites
- Include competitor sites
- Include industry publications

OBSERVATION VS TARGETING

OBSERVATION MODE
- Ads shown to all users
- Bid adjustments applied to selected audiences
- Good for: Learning audience value

TARGETING MODE
- Ads shown only to selected audiences
- Restricts reach
- Good for: Proven high-value audiences

SECTION 4 - MEASUREMENT AND OPTIMIZATION
================================================================================

CONVERSION TRACKING

CONVERSION ACTIONS

PRIMARY CONVERSIONS
- Used for bidding optimization
- Should be core business outcome
- One primary action per campaign type recommended

SECONDARY CONVERSIONS
- Tracked but not used for bidding
- Micro-conversions and engagement
- Useful for funnel analysis

ENHANCED CONVERSIONS
- First-party data sent with conversions
- Improves match rate 5-15%
- Required for future-proof measurement
- Setup: Tag-based or API

CONVERSION WINDOW SETTINGS
- Click-through: 30-90 days typical
- View-through: 1-7 days typical
- Match window to actual purchase cycle

ATTRIBUTION MODELS

DATA-DRIVEN ATTRIBUTION (DDA)
- Google recommended default
- Uses machine learning for credit allocation
- Requires sufficient conversion volume

LAST-CLICK
- All credit to final interaction
- Undervalues upper-funnel
- Use for comparison only

POSITION-BASED
- 40% first, 40% last, 20% middle
- Better balance than last-click
- Alternative when DDA unavailable

OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

WEEKLY TASKS
- Review search terms, add negatives
- Check asset performance ratings
- Monitor pacing and budget utilization
- Review auction insights for competitive shifts

MONTHLY TASKS
- Refresh audience lists
- Update seasonal bid adjustments
- Review geographic performance
- Analyze device performance

QUARTERLY TASKS
- Audit account structure
- Refresh creative assets
- Review conversion tracking setup
- Evaluate bidding strategy performance

SECTION 5 - BUDGET AND BIDDING CALCULATIONS
================================================================================

BUDGET ALLOCATION

DAILY BUDGET BEHAVIOR
- Google may spend up to 2x daily budget
- Monthly spend capped at daily x 30.4
- Plan for variability in daily spend

BUDGET CALCULATION
Daily_Budget = Monthly_Budget / 30.4

BUDGET BY CAMPAIGN TYPE
- Brand Search: 10-20% (high efficiency, protect brand)
- Non-Brand Search: 30-50% (core demand capture)
- Performance Max: 20-40% (broad reach, automation)
- Demand Gen: 10-20% (mid-funnel consideration)

IMPRESSION SHARE ANALYSIS

SEARCH IMPRESSION SHARE
Impression_Share = Impressions / Eligible_Impressions x 100

LOST IS (BUDGET)
Opportunities missed due to budget constraints.
Action: Increase budget or improve efficiency

LOST IS (RANK)
Opportunities missed due to low Ad Rank.
Action: Improve bids, quality score, or assets

TARGET IMPRESSION SHARE
- Brand terms: Target 90%+ IS
- Non-brand: Balance IS with efficiency
- Monitor competitor IS in auction insights

QUALITY SCORE FACTORS

COMPONENTS
- Expected CTR: Historical and predicted CTR
- Ad Relevance: Keyword to ad copy alignment
- Landing Page Experience: Relevance and usability

QUALITY SCORE IMPACT
- QS 7+: Achieving expected performance
- QS 5-6: Improvement opportunities exist
- QS 1-4: Significant issues require attention

IMPROVEMENT LEVERS
- Improve ad copy relevance to keywords
- Test new responsive search ad variations
- Optimize landing page content and speed
- Refine keyword to ad group mapping

SECTION 6 - AGENT APPLICATION GUIDANCE
================================================================================

CAMPAIGN TYPE SELECTION

FOR LEAD GENERATION
- Search (non-brand) for intent capture
- Search (brand) for demand protection
- Performance Max for scale
- Demand Gen for consideration

FOR ECOMMERCE
- Shopping/PMax for product visibility
- Search (brand) for demand protection
- Search (non-brand) for category capture
- Demand Gen for new customer acquisition

FOR AWARENESS
- YouTube (via PMax or standalone)
- Demand Gen for visual storytelling
- Display for reach extension

BUDGET RECOMMENDATIONS

MINIMUM VIABLE BUDGETS
- Search: $3,000/month minimum for learning
- Performance Max: $5,000/month minimum
- Demand Gen: $3,000/month minimum

SCALING THRESHOLDS
- Search: Scale when CPA stable and IS below 70%
- PMax: Scale when hitting conversion targets
- Demand Gen: Scale based on assisted conversions

WARNING SIGNS
- Impression share declining without bid changes
- CPA rising more than 20% month-over-month
- Conversion volume dropping despite stable spend
- Search terms report showing irrelevant queries
