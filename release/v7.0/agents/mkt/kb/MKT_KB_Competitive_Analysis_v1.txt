COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS FRAMEWORKS

PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document provides comprehensive guidance for conducting competitive analysis that informs marketing strategy development. Effective competitive analysis reveals opportunities for differentiation and informs positioning pricing and marketing investment decisions.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS FUNDAMENTALS

DEFINITION AND VALUE

Competitive Analysis Purpose
- Understand competitive landscape dynamics
- Identify differentiation opportunities
- Inform positioning and messaging
- Guide marketing investment decisions
- Anticipate competitive responses

Analysis Outputs
- Competitive positioning maps
- Strength weakness assessments
- Competitive response predictions
- Market opportunity identification
- Strategic recommendation development

COMPETITIVE SET DEFINITION

Direct Competitors
- Same product category
- Same target audience
- Same use case
- Head-to-head competition

Indirect Competitors
- Different product category
- Same customer need
- Alternative solution
- Substitute competition

Potential Competitors
- Adjacent category players
- Emerging market entrants
- Technology disruptors
- Vertical integration threats

COMPETITIVE RESPONSE STRATEGY

DEFENSIVE STRATEGIES

Position Defense
- Strengthen current position
- Reinforce reasons to believe
- Build switching barriers
- Maintain share of voice
- Protect key accounts

Preemptive Defense
- Fill market gaps proactively
- Launch before competitors
- Secure key partnerships
- Lock in customer commitments
- Build capability advantages

Counter-Offensive Defense
- Attack competitor weakness
- Challenge on their strength
- Reframe competitive comparison
- Redirect customer attention
- Create doubt about competitor

OFFENSIVE STRATEGIES

Frontal Attack
- Direct challenge on strength
- Superior resources required
- Clear advantage needed
- High risk high reward
- Market leader appropriate

Flank Attack
- Attack undefended segment
- Geographic or segment focus
- Avoid direct confrontation
- Build position gradually
- Expand from beachhead

Encirclement Attack
- Multi-front offensive
- Superior resources required
- Overwhelm competitor
- Block expansion options
- Force defensive posture

Bypass Attack
- Avoid direct competition
- Diversify into new areas
- Technology leapfrog
- Geographic expansion
- Unrelated diversification

Guerrilla Attack
- Small targeted actions
- Resource-efficient approach
- Selective market focus
- Hit and run tactics
- Annoyance and distraction
