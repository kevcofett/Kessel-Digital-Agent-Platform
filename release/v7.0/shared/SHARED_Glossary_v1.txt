SECTION 1 - CORE ADVERTISING TERMS
================================================================================

IMPRESSION
Single instance of an ad being displayed to a user. Counted when ad renders in browser or app, regardless of whether user notices or engages.

VIEWABLE IMPRESSION
Impression meeting industry viewability standards. Display requires 50% of pixels visible for 1 second. Video requires 50% of pixels visible for 2 continuous seconds.

REACH
Number of unique individuals exposed to advertising at least once during specified time period. Distinct from impressions which count total exposures.

FREQUENCY
Average number of times each unique user sees an ad during campaign period. Calculated as total impressions divided by unique reach.

CLICK
User action of selecting an ad to navigate to destination. Includes various click types depending on ad format and platform.

CONVERSION
Completed desired action following ad exposure. Examples include purchases, form submissions, sign-ups, downloads, or other business outcomes.

ATTRIBUTION
Process of assigning credit for conversions to specific touchpoints in the customer journey. Methods include last-click, first-click, linear, position-based, and data-driven.

SECTION 2 - PERFORMANCE METRICS
================================================================================

ROAS (RETURN ON AD SPEND)
Measure of advertising profitability. Correct formula: (Revenue - Spend) / Spend. Represents actual return percentage on investment.

CPA (COST PER ACQUISITION)
Average cost to acquire one conversion. Total spend divided by number of conversions. Also called Cost Per Action or Cost Per Conversion.

CPM (COST PER MILLE)
Cost per thousand impressions. Standard pricing model for display and video advertising. Calculated as (Spend / Impressions) x 1000.

CPC (COST PER CLICK)
Average cost for each click. Total spend divided by total clicks. Primary metric for search advertising and some social formats.

CTR (CLICK-THROUGH RATE)
Percentage of impressions resulting in clicks. Calculated as (Clicks / Impressions) x 100. Indicates ad relevance and creative effectiveness.

CVR (CONVERSION RATE)
Percentage of clicks or visits resulting in conversions. Calculated as (Conversions / Clicks) x 100. Indicates landing page and offer effectiveness.

CPV (COST PER VIEW)
Cost for each video view. Definition of view varies by platform, typically 30 seconds or completion for shorter videos.

VCR (VIDEO COMPLETION RATE)
Percentage of video starts that play to completion. Key metric for video advertising effectiveness.

ENGAGEMENT RATE
Total engagements (likes, comments, shares, saves) divided by impressions or reach. Measures content resonance with audience.

SECTION 3 - CAMPAIGN TYPES AND OBJECTIVES
================================================================================

BRAND AWARENESS
Campaign objective focused on maximizing reach and impression volume to increase brand recognition among target audience.

CONSIDERATION
Mid-funnel objective driving engagement, video views, or website traffic to build product or service familiarity.

CONVERSION
Lower-funnel objective optimizing for specific actions such as purchases, leads, or sign-ups.

RETARGETING
Strategy of serving ads to users who previously interacted with brand website, app, or content. Also called remarketing.

PROSPECTING
Strategy of reaching new audiences who have not previously engaged with brand. Opposite of retargeting.

SEQUENTIAL MESSAGING
Strategy of delivering different creative messages to same user over time in planned sequence to move through consideration stages.

================================================================================
SECTION 4 - BUYING METHODS
================================================================================

PROGRAMMATIC
Automated buying and selling of digital advertising inventory through technology platforms. Includes RTB, PMPs, and programmatic guaranteed.

RTB (REAL-TIME BIDDING)
Programmatic auction method where impressions are bought and sold individually in real-time through automated systems.

PMP (PRIVATE MARKETPLACE)
Invitation-only programmatic marketplace where select buyers access premium inventory at negotiated rates. Also called Private Auction.

PROGRAMMATIC GUARANTEED
Automated direct buy with fixed CPM and guaranteed inventory. Combines programmatic efficiency with traditional guarantee.

DIRECT BUY
Non-programmatic purchase negotiated directly with publisher. Typically involves fixed rates and guaranteed placements.

MANAGED SERVICE
Buying method where DSP or agency team handles all campaign execution, optimization, and reporting on behalf of advertiser.

SELF-SERVICE
Buying method where advertiser directly operates platform interface to set up, manage, and optimize campaigns.

================================================================================
SECTION 5 - TARGETING METHODS
================================================================================

DEMOGRAPHIC TARGETING
Targeting based on user characteristics such as age, gender, income, education, or household composition.

GEOGRAPHIC TARGETING
Targeting based on user location. Includes country, region, DMA, city, zip code, or radius targeting.

BEHAVIORAL TARGETING
Targeting based on user online behaviors such as browsing history, purchase patterns, or content consumption.

CONTEXTUAL TARGETING
Targeting based on content being consumed rather than user data. Ad placed alongside relevant content topics.

INTEREST TARGETING
Targeting users based on expressed or inferred interests derived from browsing behavior, content engagement, or declared preferences.

LOOKALIKE AUDIENCE
Audience of users who share characteristics with seed audience such as existing customers. Also called similar audience or audience expansion.

CUSTOM AUDIENCE
Audience built from first-party data such as customer lists, website visitors, or app users.

FIRST-PARTY DATA
Data collected directly by advertiser from their own properties and customer interactions.

SECOND-PARTY DATA
Another organization's first-party data accessed through partnership agreement.

THIRD-PARTY DATA
Data aggregated from multiple sources and sold by data providers. Subject to increasing privacy restrictions.

================================================================================
SECTION 6 - AD FORMATS
================================================================================

DISPLAY
Static or animated image ads served across websites and apps. Common sizes include 300x250, 728x90, 160x600, and 300x600.

NATIVE
Ad format matching form and function of surrounding content. Designed to appear as organic content rather than traditional advertising.

VIDEO PRE-ROLL
Video ad playing before primary video content. Typically 15 or 30 seconds with skip option after 5 seconds.

VIDEO MID-ROLL
Video ad playing during breaks in longer-form video content.

VIDEO OUT-STREAM
Video ad appearing outside traditional video player, typically within text content or social feeds.

CONNECTED TV (CTV)
Video advertising delivered through internet-connected television devices including smart TVs, streaming devices, and gaming consoles.

AUDIO
Advertising delivered through streaming audio platforms such as music services and podcasts.

RICH MEDIA
Display ads with advanced interactive features such as video, animation, games, or expandable formats.

INTERSTITIAL
Full-screen ad appearing between content pages or during app transitions.

REWARDED VIDEO
Video ad format where users opt-in to watch in exchange for in-app rewards or benefits.

CAROUSEL
Multi-card ad format allowing users to swipe through multiple images or videos within single ad unit.

STORIES
Full-screen vertical ad format appearing between user stories on social platforms.

================================================================================
SECTION 7 - PLATFORMS AND TECHNOLOGY
================================================================================

DSP (DEMAND-SIDE PLATFORM)
Technology platform enabling advertisers to buy digital advertising inventory across multiple exchanges and networks through single interface.

SSP (SUPPLY-SIDE PLATFORM)
Technology platform enabling publishers to sell their advertising inventory to multiple demand sources simultaneously.

AD EXCHANGE
Digital marketplace connecting DSPs and SSPs where advertising inventory is bought and sold through real-time auctions.

DMP (DATA MANAGEMENT PLATFORM)
Technology platform for collecting, organizing, and activating audience data for targeting and personalization.

CDP (CUSTOMER DATA PLATFORM)
Platform creating unified customer database from multiple sources with persistent customer profiles for marketing activation.

AD SERVER
Technology serving ads to websites or apps, tracking delivery and performance, and managing creative assets.

VERIFICATION
Third-party technology measuring whether ads were delivered as specified including viewability, brand safety, and fraud detection.

IDENTITY RESOLUTION
Process of connecting user data across devices, channels, and platforms to create unified user profiles.

CLEAN ROOM
Secure environment enabling multiple parties to match and analyze data without sharing underlying personal data.

================================================================================
SECTION 8 - MEASUREMENT TERMS
================================================================================

LIFT STUDY
Controlled experiment measuring incremental impact of advertising by comparing exposed group to control holdout group.

BRAND LIFT
Measurement of advertising impact on brand metrics such as awareness, consideration, favorability, or purchase intent.

CONVERSION LIFT
Measurement of advertising impact on conversion rates by comparing test and control groups.

INCREMENTALITY
Measurement approach determining conversions that would not have occurred without advertising exposure.

MTA (MULTI-TOUCH ATTRIBUTION)
Attribution methodology assigning fractional credit to multiple touchpoints in conversion path.

MMM (MEDIA MIX MODELING)
Statistical analysis measuring impact of marketing activities on business outcomes using aggregate data over time.

UNIFIED MEASUREMENT
Approach combining multiple measurement methodologies (MTA, MMM, incrementality) for comprehensive view.

LAST-CLICK ATTRIBUTION
Attribution model assigning 100% credit to final touchpoint before conversion.

FIRST-CLICK ATTRIBUTION
Attribution model assigning 100% credit to initial touchpoint in conversion path.

DATA-DRIVEN ATTRIBUTION
Attribution model using machine learning to distribute credit based on actual contribution patterns.

================================================================================
SECTION 9 - BRAND SAFETY AND QUALITY
================================================================================

BRAND SAFETY
Protection of brand reputation by preventing ads from appearing alongside inappropriate or harmful content.

BRAND SUITABILITY
Extension of brand safety considering brand-specific context preferences beyond universally harmful content.

VIEWABILITY
Measurement of whether ad had opportunity to be seen. Industry standard requires minimum pixel percentage visible for minimum time.

IVT (INVALID TRAFFIC)
Non-human traffic including bots, crawlers, and fraudulent activity. Divided into GIVT (general) and SIVT (sophisticated).

AD FRAUD
Deliberate activity generating false impressions, clicks, or conversions for financial gain.

MADE-FOR-ADVERTISING (MFA)
Low-quality websites created primarily to generate ad revenue rather than provide genuine content value.

BLOCK LIST
List of domains, apps, or content categories excluded from receiving ad delivery.

ALLOW LIST
List of approved domains, apps, or placements specifically authorized for ad delivery. Also called inclusion list.

SECTION 10 - PRIVACY AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

GDPR (GENERAL DATA PROTECTION REGULATION)
European Union regulation governing collection and use of personal data. Requires explicit consent and data subject rights.

CCPA (CALIFORNIA CONSUMER PRIVACY ACT)
California law providing consumers rights over personal information including right to know, delete, and opt-out of sale.

CONSENT MANAGEMENT
Systems and processes for obtaining, storing, and honoring user privacy preferences and consent choices.

PRIVACY SANDBOX
Google initiative developing privacy-preserving technologies to replace third-party cookies while maintaining advertising functionality.

TOPICS API
Google Privacy Sandbox proposal for interest-based advertising using browser-determined topic categories.

ATTRIBUTION REPORTING API
Google Privacy Sandbox proposal for measuring conversions without cross-site tracking.

PROTECTED AUDIENCES API
Google Privacy Sandbox proposal for remarketing without third-party cookies. Formerly known as FLEDGE.

SERVER-SIDE TRACKING
Data collection method sending data to server before forwarding to analytics or advertising platforms. Provides more control and reliability.

================================================================================
SECTION 11 - OPTIMIZATION TERMS
================================================================================

A/B TESTING
Controlled experiment comparing two versions to determine which performs better. Also called split testing.

MULTIVARIATE TESTING
Testing multiple variables simultaneously to identify best-performing combinations.

BID OPTIMIZATION
Automated or manual adjustment of bid amounts to maximize performance within constraints.

BUDGET OPTIMIZATION
Distribution of budget across campaigns, ad groups, or time periods to maximize results.

CREATIVE OPTIMIZATION
Process of improving ad creative elements through testing and iteration.

AUDIENCE OPTIMIZATION
Refinement of targeting parameters based on performance data to improve efficiency.

PACING
Rate at which campaign spends budget over time. Can be accelerated, standard, or custom.

FREQUENCY CAPPING
Limit on number of times individual user sees specific ad within time period.

DAYPARTING
Scheduling ad delivery during specific times of day or days of week based on performance patterns.

================================================================================
SECTION 12 - AGENT-SPECIFIC TERMS
================================================================================

ORCHESTRATOR (ORC)
Central routing agent determining which specialist agent should handle user query based on intent and context.

ANALYST (ANL)
Agent specializing in data analysis, forecasting, media mix modeling, and performance interpretation.

AUDIENCE (AUD)
Agent specializing in audience strategy, segmentation, targeting recommendations, and identity resolution.

CHANNEL (CHA)
Agent specializing in channel strategy, platform selection, and media mix recommendations.

SPEND OPTIMIZATION (SPO)
Agent specializing in budget allocation, spend efficiency, and investment optimization.

PERFORMANCE (PRF)
Agent specializing in measurement strategy, attribution, testing methodologies, and privacy compliance.

DOCUMENT (DOC)
Agent specializing in creating reports, presentations, and other formatted deliverables.

KNOWLEDGE BASE (KB)
Collection of reference documents providing agent with domain expertise and factual information.

HANDOFF
Transfer of conversation from one agent to another based on query requirements or workflow progression.

SESSION CONTEXT
Information about current conversation including user inputs, agent responses, and derived insights.
