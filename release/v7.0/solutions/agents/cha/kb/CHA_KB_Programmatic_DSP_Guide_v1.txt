SECTION 1 - DSP FUNDAMENTALS
================================================================================

DSP DEFINITION AND ROLE

DEMAND-SIDE PLATFORM
A DSP enables advertisers to purchase digital ad inventory programmatically across multiple exchanges and publishers from a single interface.

CORE DSP FUNCTIONS
- Real-time bidding on impressions
- Audience targeting and segmentation
- Campaign management and optimization
- Reporting and analytics
- Frequency management across inventory

MAJOR DSPS

THE TRADE DESK (TTD)
- Market leader for independent DSPs
- Strong CTV and audio capabilities
- Unified ID 2.0 for identity
- Kokai AI platform for optimization
- Best for: Agencies, mid-to-large advertisers

GOOGLE DV360 (DISPLAY AND VIDEO 360)
- Google ecosystem integration
- YouTube access and Google audiences
- Strong programmatic guaranteed
- Best for: Google-centric advertisers, YouTube buyers

AMAZON DSP
- Amazon shopper data (exclusive)
- Retail media integration
- On and off Amazon inventory
- Best for: Ecommerce, CPG brands

XANDR (MICROSOFT)
- Microsoft ecosystem integration
- LinkedIn targeting available
- Strong in political and CTV
- Best for: Microsoft advertisers, B2B

YAHOO DSP
- Native and content integration
- Strong mobile capabilities
- Omnichannel identity
- Best for: Native advertising, mobile focus

SECTION 2 - INVENTORY TYPES AND ACCESS
================================================================================

OPEN EXCHANGE (RTB)

DEFINITION
Real-time auction where any eligible buyer can bid on available impressions.

CHARACTERISTICS
- Lowest cost access
- Highest scale
- Variable quality
- Limited transparency

TYPICAL CPMS
- Display: $1-5 CPM
- Video: $8-20 CPM
- Native: $3-10 CPM
- CTV: $15-35 CPM

BEST PRACTICES
- Layer verification vendors
- Use inclusion lists
- Apply brand safety controls
- Monitor for fraud

PRIVATE MARKETPLACE (PMP)

DEFINITION
Invitation-only auctions with select publishers offering premium inventory.

CHARACTERISTICS
- Higher quality guaranteed
- Better transparency
- Floor prices set by publisher
- Curated inventory packages

DEAL TYPES

PRIVATE AUCTION
- Floor price set, not guaranteed
- Still competitive bidding
- Higher floors than open exchange

PREFERRED DEAL
- Fixed price, not guaranteed
- First look before auction
- No commitment required

PMP MANAGEMENT
- Negotiate floor prices
- Request specific placements
- Demand viewability guarantees
- Review performance monthly

PROGRAMMATIC GUARANTEED (PG)

DEFINITION
Automated direct buy with guaranteed impressions at fixed price.

CHARACTERISTICS
- Reserved inventory
- Fixed CPM
- Guaranteed delivery
- Premium placements

ADVANTAGES
- Budget certainty
- Priority access
- Premium environments
- Simplified buying

WHEN TO USE
- Tentpole campaigns
- Specific publisher targeting
- Premium video and CTV
- Brand-critical placements

DEAL ID MANAGEMENT

DEAL STRUCTURE
- Deal ID: Unique identifier for inventory package
- Floor price: Minimum bid accepted
- Inventory: Specific sites, placements, formats
- Duration: Active period for deal

DEAL OPTIMIZATION
- Monitor win rates by deal
- Compare deal vs open performance
- Negotiate based on performance data
- Sunset underperforming deals

SECTION 3 - TARGETING CAPABILITIES
================================================================================

FIRST-PARTY DATA ACTIVATION

CRM DATA ONBOARDING
- Upload hashed customer files
- Match to device IDs and cookies
- Typical match rates: 40-60%
- Refresh monthly for accuracy

WEBSITE AUDIENCES
- Pixel-based collection
- Segment by behavior
- Recency windows customizable
- Cross-device extension available

AUDIENCE SEGMENTATION
- High-value customers
- Recent purchasers
- Product category interests
- Engagement level tiers

THIRD-PARTY DATA

DATA PROVIDERS
- Oracle Data Cloud
- Lotame
- LiveRamp
- Experian
- Nielsen

DATA CATEGORIES
- Demographics
- Purchase behavior
- Interests and intents
- B2B firmographics
- Financial indicators

DATA COSTS
- CPM-based pricing
- Range: $0.50-5.00 CPM
- Stacking multiplies costs
- Evaluate incrementality

CONTEXTUAL TARGETING

CONTEXTUAL APPROACH
- Target content, not users
- Privacy-friendly
- No cookies required
- Real-time page analysis

CONTEXTUAL VENDORS
- Oracle Contextual
- Peer39
- DoubleVerify Semantic Science
- IAS Context Control

TARGETING METHODS
- Keyword targeting
- Category targeting
- Sentiment targeting
- Custom contextual segments

BEST PRACTICES
- Combine with other signals
- Test category vs keyword
- Include negative contexts
- Monitor performance by context

LOOKALIKE AND PREDICTIVE

LOOKALIKE MODELING
- Seed from first-party data
- DSP builds similar audience
- Scale vs similarity trade-off
- Refresh seeds quarterly

PREDICTIVE AUDIENCES
- DSP predicts likely converters
- Based on conversion patterns
- Requires sufficient history
- Often outperforms static segments

SECTION 4 - CTV AND VIDEO STRATEGY
================================================================================

CTV INVENTORY ACCESS

INVENTORY SOURCES
- Publisher direct apps
- SSPs with CTV supply
- vMVPDs (streaming bundles)
- AVOD services

MAJOR CTV SOURCES
- Hulu
- Peacock
- Paramount Plus
- Roku
- Samsung TV Plus
- Tubi
- Pluto TV

CTV BUYING CONSIDERATIONS
- Limited frequency capping across apps
- Household vs user targeting
- Completion rate optimization
- Cross-device attribution challenges

CTV CPMS
- Premium content: $30-50 CPM
- General AVOD: $15-30 CPM
- FAST channels: $12-25 CPM

ONLINE VIDEO (OLV)

INVENTORY TYPES
- In-stream: Before, during, after content
- Out-stream: In-content, in-feed
- In-banner video: Display with video

QUALITY CONSIDERATIONS
- Viewability standards: 50% in view, 2 seconds
- Completion rate targets: 70%+ for 15s
- Sound on vs off environments
- Above fold vs below fold

VIDEO FORMATS
- 15 seconds: Standard pre-roll
- 30 seconds: Premium inventory
- 6 seconds: Bumper ads
- Non-skippable vs skippable

FREQUENCY MANAGEMENT FOR VIDEO

FREQUENCY GOALS
- CTV: 3-7 exposures per week per household
- OLV: 2-4 exposures per week per user
- Cross-channel: Consider total video exposure

FREQUENCY CHALLENGES
- CTV lacks cookies for frequency
- Household vs individual frequency
- Cross-publisher coordination limited

FREQUENCY SOLUTIONS
- Use identity solutions (UID2, RampID)
- Set conservative caps per publisher
- Monitor reach vs frequency reports
- Accept some over-frequency in CTV

SECTION 5 - OPTIMIZATION LEVERS
================================================================================

BID OPTIMIZATION

BIDDING STRATEGIES

FIXED BID
- Set maximum bid manually
- Full control, requires monitoring
- Good for: Testing, specific inventory

DYNAMIC BIDDING
- DSP adjusts based on likelihood to convert
- Bid up for high-value impressions
- Bid down for low-value
- Good for: Performance campaigns

GOAL-BASED BIDDING
- Set CPA or ROAS target
- Algorithm optimizes to goal
- Requires conversion data
- Good for: Mature campaigns

BID SHADING
- Automatically reduces bids in first-price auctions
- Prevents overpaying
- Most DSPs apply automatically
- Monitor clearing prices vs bids

PACING CONTROLS

PACING OPTIONS
- Even pacing: Spread evenly
- Front-loaded: Spend quickly
- Accelerated: No pacing limits
- Custom: Day-parting patterns

PACING BEST PRACTICES
- Even pacing for most campaigns
- Front-load for short flights
- Accelerate only if underdelivering
- Monitor daily for adjustments

FREQUENCY OPTIMIZATION

FREQUENCY CAPS
- Per user per day
- Per user per week
- Per user lifetime
- Per household (CTV)

FREQUENCY RECOMMENDATIONS
- Awareness: 7-12 per month
- Consideration: 5-8 per month
- Conversion: 3-5 per month

RECENCY TARGETING
- Weight recent visitors higher
- Decrease bids over time
- Fresh audiences more valuable

INVENTORY OPTIMIZATION

INCLUSION LISTS
- Approved sites and apps only
- Highest quality, lowest scale
- Require for brand-sensitive

EXCLUSION LISTS
- Block specific domains
- Block categories
- Block made-for-advertising sites

VIEWABILITY OPTIMIZATION
- Set viewability thresholds
- Bid more for viewable inventory
- Accept trade-off with scale

SECTION 6 - VERIFICATION AND BRAND SAFETY
================================================================================

VERIFICATION VENDORS

DOUBLECHECK (IAS)
- Pre-bid brand safety
- Viewability measurement
- Fraud detection
- Contextual targeting

DOUBLEVERIFY
- Similar capabilities to IAS
- Authentic Brand Safety
- Attention metrics
- Custom brand suitability

ORACLE MOAT
- Attention measurement
- Viewability
- Cross-platform measurement
- IVT detection

BRAND SAFETY CONTROLS

CATEGORY BLOCKING
- Standard: Adult, violence, illegal
- Custom: Sensitive topics for brand
- Political: Optional exclusion
- News: Consider during crises

KEYWORD BLOCKING
- Block pages with specific words
- Risk of over-blocking
- Review blocked inventory regularly
- Whitelist high-value sites

DOMAIN BLOCKING
- Exclude specific sites
- Made-for-advertising sites
- Low-quality publishers
- Update quarterly

FRAUD PREVENTION

IVT TYPES
- GIVT: General Invalid Traffic (bots, crawlers)
- SIVT: Sophisticated Invalid Traffic (fraud)
- Pre-bid filtering removes most GIVT
- Post-bid analysis catches SIVT

FRAUD INDICATORS
- Abnormally high CTR (above 1% display)
- Unusual geographic patterns
- Low time-on-site post-click
- Suspicious conversion patterns

FRAUD MITIGATION
- Use pre-bid IVT filtering
- Apply ads.txt enforcement
- Monitor for anomalies
- Require verification vendors

SECTION 7 - MEASUREMENT AND ATTRIBUTION
================================================================================

CONVERSION TRACKING

PIXEL IMPLEMENTATION
- Place on conversion pages
- Pass conversion value
- Set attribution windows
- Dedupe across platforms

ATTRIBUTION WINDOWS
- View-through: 1-7 days typical
- Click-through: 7-30 days typical
- Match to purchase cycle

CONVERSION TYPES
- Post-click conversions
- Post-view conversions
- Assisted conversions
- Cross-device conversions

CROSS-DEVICE MEASUREMENT

IDENTITY SOLUTIONS
- Unified ID 2.0 (open source)
- LiveRamp RampID
- DSP proprietary graphs
- Publisher authenticated traffic

CROSS-DEVICE CHALLENGES
- Cookie deprecation impact
- Walled garden limitations
- Match rate variability
- Attribution complexity

INCREMENTALITY MEASUREMENT

TEST APPROACHES
- Geo holdout testing
- Ghost bidding
- PSA testing
- Platform incrementality studies

TYPICAL INCREMENTALITY
- Prospecting: 20-40% of attributed
- Retargeting: 30-50% of attributed
- CTV: Often higher incrementality

SECTION 8 - AGENT APPLICATION GUIDANCE
================================================================================

DSP SELECTION GUIDANCE

CHOOSE THE TRADE DESK FOR
- Agency or multi-brand accounts
- CTV and audio focus
- Identity-forward strategy
- Independence from walled gardens

CHOOSE DV360 FOR
- Google ecosystem alignment
- YouTube buying requirements
- Programmatic guaranteed focus
- Google audience access

CHOOSE AMAZON DSP FOR
- Ecommerce advertisers
- Amazon seller data
- Retail media strategy
- Shopper behavior targeting

BUDGET RECOMMENDATIONS

MINIMUM BUDGETS
- Display: $10,000/month minimum
- Video: $15,000/month minimum
- CTV: $25,000/month minimum

BUDGET ALLOCATION
- Open exchange: 30-50% (scale)
- PMP: 30-50% (quality)
- Programmatic guaranteed: 10-30% (premium)

DEAL STRUCTURE GUIDANCE
- Start with open exchange testing
- Identify performing inventory
- Negotiate PMPs with performing publishers
- Add PG for tentpole moments

OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

DAILY
- Pacing check
- Anomaly review
- Win rate monitoring

WEEKLY
- Creative performance
- Audience performance
- Deal performance
- Frequency distribution

MONTHLY
- Verification reports
- Vendor fee analysis
- Attribution review
- Strategy refinement

WARNING SIGNS
- Win rate below 15% (bid too low)
- Win rate above 50% (overpaying)
- CPM variance above 30%
- Viewability below 50%
- IVT rate above 3%
