BUSINESS CASE DOCUMENT STRUCTURE

OVERVIEW

The business case document provides comprehensive justification for an investment decision. It presents the problem, proposed solution, financial analysis, risks, and recommendation in a format suitable for executive approval.

DOCUMENT OUTLINE

Title Page
- Document title: Business Case for [Initiative Name]
- Version number and date
- Prepared by and prepared for
- Confidentiality classification

Executive Summary - Target One Page

The executive summary must stand alone as a complete argument

Opening Statement
- State the recommendation clearly in first sentence
- Example: We recommend proceeding with [initiative] requiring [investment] to achieve [primary benefit].

Problem or Opportunity
- Two to three sentences describing the business driver
- Quantify the cost of inaction or value of opportunity

Proposed Solution
- Brief description of what is proposed
- Key differentiators from alternatives

Financial Summary
- Investment required (capital and operating)
- Expected return (NPV, IRR, payback)
- Key financial metrics in simple format

Risk Summary
- Top two to three risks with mitigation approach
- Overall risk assessment

Recommendation
- Clear call to action
- Timeline for decision and next steps

Problem Statement Section

Context
- Business background and strategic context
- How this connects to organizational priorities
- Market or competitive factors driving urgency

Problem Definition
- Specific problem being addressed
- Root cause analysis if applicable
- Scope of impact (who is affected, how much)

Cost of Inaction
- Quantified impact of not addressing problem
- Trend analysis showing deterioration
- Competitive disadvantage if applicable

Proposed Solution Section

Solution Description
- What will be done in clear terms
- Technology, process, or organizational changes
- Scope boundaries (what is included and excluded)

Solution Components
- Major workstreams or phases
- Key deliverables from each component
- Dependencies and integration points

Alternatives Considered
- List of options evaluated
- Brief description of each option
- Reason each alternative was not selected

Why This Solution
- Key advantages of recommended solution
- Fit with organizational capabilities
- Risk profile compared to alternatives

Financial Analysis Section

Investment Summary
- Capital expenditure by category
- Operating expenditure by year
- Implementation costs
- Contingency and assumptions

Benefit Quantification
- Revenue benefits (new revenue, pricing improvement)
- Cost benefits (savings, avoidance, efficiency)
- Qualitative benefits with proxy valuation if possible
- Timing of benefit realization

Financial Metrics
- Net Present Value with discount rate stated
- Internal Rate of Return
- Payback Period (simple and discounted)
- Return on Investment

Cash Flow Projection
- Year-by-year cash flow table
- Investment timeline
- Benefit realization timeline
- Net cash flow by period

Sensitivity Analysis
- Key assumptions and ranges
- NPV sensitivity to major variables
- Scenario analysis (base, optimistic, pessimistic)
- Break-even analysis

Risk Assessment Section

Risk Identification
- List of key risks with descriptions
- Risk categories (financial, operational, technical, external)
- Risk source and potential trigger

Risk Analysis
- Probability assessment (High, Medium, Low)
- Impact assessment (High, Medium, Low)
- Risk priority matrix

Risk Mitigation
- Mitigation strategy for each major risk
- Contingency plans for residual risk
- Risk monitoring approach

Implementation Approach Section

Phasing Strategy
- Phase definitions with scope and objectives
- Timeline for each phase
- Go/no-go criteria between phases

Resource Requirements
- Team structure and key roles
- Internal versus external resources
- Skills and capabilities required

Timeline
- High-level milestone schedule
- Critical path and dependencies
- Key decision points

Governance
- Steering committee structure
- Decision rights and escalation
- Reporting and review cadence

Recommendation Section

Summary of Case
- Restate key points supporting recommendation
- Confirm alignment with strategic priorities
- Note confidence level in analysis

Recommendation Statement
- Clear, specific recommendation
- Investment amount and timing
- Expected outcomes and timeline

Approval Request
- What approval is being sought
- Authority level required
- Timeline for decision

Next Steps
- Immediate actions upon approval
- Key milestones in first 90 days
- Dependencies on other decisions

Appendices

Appendix A - Detailed Financial Model
- Complete cash flow model
- Assumption documentation
- Calculation methodology

Appendix B - Research and Sources
- Source citations for market data
- Benchmark sources
- Research quality summary

Appendix C - Detailed Risk Register
- Complete risk inventory
- Risk scoring detail
- Mitigation plan detail

Appendix D - Stakeholder Analysis
- Key stakeholders and roles
- Communication plan
- Change impact assessment

IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP STRUCTURE

OVERVIEW

The implementation roadmap provides a strategic plan for executing an approved initiative. It translates the business case into actionable phases with clear milestones, dependencies, and success metrics.

DOCUMENT OUTLINE

Title Page
- Document title: Implementation Roadmap for [Initiative Name]
- Version number and date
- Prepared by and prepared for
- Status (Draft, Final, Approved)

Executive Overview - Target One Page

Strategic Context
- Initiative objective and business case reference
- Alignment with organizational strategy
- Scope summary

Approach Summary
- High-level phasing strategy
- Timeline overview
- Investment and resource summary

Success Vision
- End-state description
- Key outcomes and measures
- Critical success factors

Current State Summary

As-Is Assessment
- Current capabilities and processes
- Technology and system landscape
- Organizational structure and skills
- Performance baseline metrics

Pain Points and Gaps
- Key issues with current state
- Gap analysis summary
- Priority areas for improvement

Readiness Assessment
- Organizational change readiness
- Technical readiness
- Resource availability

Future State Vision

Target State Description
- Desired capabilities and processes
- Technology and system target architecture
- Organizational and skills target state
- Expected performance improvements

Design Principles
- Guiding principles for solution design
- Standards and constraints
- Integration and interoperability requirements

Success Metrics
- Key performance indicators
- Measurement approach
- Targets and thresholds

Phased Implementation Approach

Phase Overview
- Phase names and objectives
- Duration and timing
- Investment by phase
- Phase dependencies

For Each Phase Include

Phase Objectives
- What this phase accomplishes
- Scope boundaries for this phase
- Entry criteria from previous phase

Key Deliverables
- Major outputs from this phase
- Acceptance criteria
- Delivery timeline

Milestones
- Key milestone dates
- Milestone definitions
- Dependencies and constraints

Resource Requirements
- Team composition for this phase
- Skills and capabilities needed
- External support required

Phase Exit Criteria
- Conditions to proceed to next phase
- Quality gates
- Approval requirements

Detailed Timeline

Milestone Schedule
- Complete milestone list
- Dates and durations
- Dependencies mapped

Workstream View
- Parallel workstreams
- Integration points
- Resource contention periods

Critical Path
- Longest path through project
- Float analysis
- Acceleration opportunities

Resource Plan

Team Structure
- Organization chart for implementation
- Roles and responsibilities
- Reporting relationships

Staffing Plan
- Resource types and counts by phase
- Internal versus external mix
- Ramp-up and ramp-down timing

Skills Matrix
- Required skills by role
- Gap assessment
- Training and development plan

Risk and Contingency

Implementation Risks
- Risks specific to implementation
- Schedule risks
- Resource risks
- Technical risks

Mitigation Strategies
- Prevention measures
- Contingency plans
- Risk triggers and responses

Contingency Budget
- Management reserve
- Contingency allocation
- Trigger for contingency use

Governance Model

Steering Committee
- Members and roles
- Meeting cadence
- Decision authority

Project Leadership
- Project manager role
- Workstream leads
- Integration management

Reporting Structure
- Status reporting frequency
- Report templates
- Escalation procedures

Change Control
- Change request process
- Impact assessment approach
- Approval thresholds

Success Measurement

Key Performance Indicators
- Leading indicators during implementation
- Lagging indicators post-implementation
- Baseline and target values

Measurement Plan
- Data collection approach
- Reporting frequency
- Review and action process

Benefits Realization
- Benefit tracking methodology
- Realization timeline
- Accountability for benefits

Appendices

Appendix A - Detailed Project Plan
- Work breakdown structure
- Task-level schedule
- Resource assignments

Appendix B - RACI Matrix
- Responsibility assignments
- By deliverable or decision
- Stakeholder coverage

Appendix C - Communication Plan
- Stakeholder communication matrix
- Message templates
- Communication calendar

Appendix D - Change Management Plan
- Impact assessment
- Training plan
- Adoption support

ASSESSMENT REPORT STRUCTURE

OVERVIEW

The assessment report presents findings from a diagnostic or evaluation engagement. It documents current state, identifies issues and opportunities, and provides prioritized recommendations.

DOCUMENT OUTLINE

Title Page
- Document title: [Topic] Assessment Report
- Version number and date
- Prepared by and prepared for
- Confidentiality classification

Executive Summary - Target Two Pages

Assessment Overview
- Purpose and scope of assessment
- Methodology used
- Timeframe of assessment activities

Key Findings
- Top five to seven findings
- Brief description and significance of each
- Findings should be specific and evidence-based

Overall Assessment
- Summary rating or maturity level if applicable
- Comparison to benchmarks or best practices
- Relative strengths and improvement areas

Priority Recommendations
- Top three to five recommendations
- Expected impact of each
- Implementation complexity

Methodology Section

Assessment Approach
- Framework or methodology used
- Standards or benchmarks applied
- Evaluation criteria

Data Collection
- Sources of information
- Interviews conducted (roles, not names)
- Documents reviewed
- Systems or processes observed

Analysis Approach
- How data was analyzed
- Scoring or rating methodology
- Validation approach

Limitations
- Scope constraints
- Data limitations
- Areas not assessed

Current State Analysis

Dimension 1: [Area Name]
- Current state description
- Supporting evidence
- Comparison to benchmark or standard
- Rating: [Strong / Adequate / Needs Improvement / Critical Gap]

Dimension 2: [Area Name]
- Current state description
- Supporting evidence
- Comparison to benchmark or standard
- Rating: [Strong / Adequate / Needs Improvement / Critical Gap]

[Continue for each dimension assessed]

Repeat structure for each assessment dimension, typically four to eight dimensions depending on scope.

Dimension Assessment Format

Description of Current State
- Objective description of what exists
- How it operates in practice
- Key characteristics observed

Evidence and Examples
- Specific examples supporting description
- Data points and metrics
- Quotes or observations (anonymized)

Gap Analysis
- Comparison to standard or best practice
- Specific gaps identified
- Magnitude of gaps

Rating and Rationale
- Overall rating for dimension
- Key factors driving rating
- Areas of relative strength and weakness

Findings and Observations

Finding Format for Each

Finding Statement
- Clear, concise statement of finding
- Specific and evidence-based
- Avoid subjective language

Supporting Evidence
- Data, observations, or quotes supporting finding
- Source references
- Confidence level

Impact Assessment
- Business impact of this finding
- Severity (Critical, High, Medium, Low)
- Urgency of addressing

Root Cause
- Why this situation exists
- Contributing factors
- Historical context if relevant

Finding Categories

Organize findings by theme or category
- Strategic findings
- Operational findings
- Technology findings
- People and organization findings
- Process findings

Finding Prioritization
- Rank findings by impact and urgency
- Create findings priority matrix
- Highlight critical findings

Recommendations Section

Recommendation Format for Each

Recommendation Statement
- Clear action recommendation
- Specific and actionable
- Verb-led statement

Rationale
- Why this recommendation
- Link to findings
- Expected benefit

Implementation Approach
- High-level approach to implement
- Key steps or phases
- Dependencies

Expected Impact
- Quantified where possible
- Qualitative benefits
- Timeline to realize impact

Complexity Assessment
- Level of effort (High, Medium, Low)
- Resource requirements
- Risk level

Priority
- Recommended priority (1, 2, 3)
- Sequencing considerations
- Quick wins versus strategic initiatives

Recommendations Summary

Priority Matrix
- Map recommendations by impact and complexity
- Identify quick wins (high impact, low complexity)
- Identify strategic initiatives (high impact, high complexity)
- Identify foundational items (enablers for other recommendations)

Sequencing
- Recommended implementation order
- Dependencies between recommendations
- Parallel versus sequential execution

Investment Estimate
- Order of magnitude investment by recommendation
- Resource requirements
- Timeline estimates

Roadmap Preview

Short-Term Actions (0-6 months)
- Quick wins and foundational items
- Immediate priorities
- Early value delivery

Medium-Term Actions (6-18 months)
- Major improvement initiatives
- Capability building
- Process transformation

Long-Term Actions (18+ months)
- Strategic transformation
- Sustained improvement
- Advanced capabilities

Appendices

Appendix A - Detailed Assessment Data
- Complete rating details by dimension
- Sub-dimension scores
- Benchmark comparisons

Appendix B - Interview Summary
- Topics covered
- Key themes by stakeholder group
- Anonymized quotes

Appendix C - Document Inventory
- Documents reviewed
- Analysis performed
- Key takeaways by document

Appendix D - Recommendation Detail
- Extended implementation guidance
- Success criteria
- Risk mitigation

TEMPLATE SELECTION LOGIC

SELECTING BUSINESS CASE TEMPLATE

Use Business Case When
- Seeking approval for significant investment
- Need to justify capital or operating expenditure
- Comparing multiple options requiring financial analysis
- Presenting to finance or executive leadership
- Formal approval process required

Business Case Variants

Full Business Case
- Investment over USD 500K
- Strategic significance
- Multiple stakeholders
- All sections required

Abbreviated Business Case
- Investment USD 100K to 500K
- Moderate complexity
- Limited stakeholder set
- Executive summary plus financial analysis

Business Case Memo
- Investment under USD 100K
- Low complexity, low risk
- One to two page format
- Financial summary only

SELECTING IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP TEMPLATE

Use Implementation Roadmap When
- Initiative approved and moving to execution
- Need detailed planning beyond business case
- Multi-phase implementation
- Multiple workstreams or teams involved
- Governance structure required

Roadmap Variants

Strategic Roadmap
- Eighteen-plus month horizon
- High-level milestones
- Phase-level detail
- Executive audience

Detailed Implementation Plan
- Six to eighteen month horizon
- Workstream detail
- Task-level planning
- Project team audience

Tactical Execution Plan
- Under six month horizon
- Sprint or iteration level
- Resource assignments
- Delivery team audience

SELECTING ASSESSMENT REPORT TEMPLATE

Use Assessment Report When
- Diagnostic or evaluation completed
- Need to present findings objectively
- Recommendations required
- Baseline for improvement established
- External or objective perspective needed

Assessment Variants

Comprehensive Assessment
- Multiple dimensions assessed
- Detailed findings and recommendations
- Full appendices
- Client or board audience

Focused Assessment
- Single dimension deep dive
- Targeted findings
- Specific recommendations
- Functional leadership audience

Quick Assessment
- Rapid diagnostic
- High-level findings
- Priority recommendations
- Working session format

COMBINING TEMPLATES

Assessment to Business Case Flow
- Assessment identifies opportunity or problem
- Business case justifies solution investment
- Reference assessment findings in problem statement
- Maintain consistency in terminology

Business Case to Roadmap Flow
- Business case approval triggers roadmap
- Roadmap expands implementation approach section
- Financial model informs resource plan
- Risks carry forward with expanded mitigation

FORMATTING STANDARDS FOR CONSULTING DELIVERABLES

PAGE LAYOUT

Standard Format
- Page size: Letter (8.5 x 11 inches) or A4 as appropriate
- Margins: 1 inch all sides
- Orientation: Portrait default, landscape for wide tables
- Headers and footers with document info and page numbers

TYPOGRAPHY

Headings
- Level 1: 16pt bold, document sections
- Level 2: 14pt bold, major subsections
- Level 3: 12pt bold, detailed subsections

Body Text
- Font: Professional sans-serif (Arial, Calibri) or serif (Times, Georgia)
- Size: 11pt or 12pt
- Line spacing: 1.15 to 1.5

VISUAL ELEMENTS

Tables
- Clear headers with shading
- Consistent alignment (numbers right, text left)
- Source citation below table
- Notes for abbreviations or special values

Charts
- Clear title describing content
- Axis labels and units
- Legend when multiple series
- Source citation
- Limit to one key message per chart

Callout Boxes
- Key findings or recommendations
- Important warnings or notes
- Summary statistics
- Visual differentiation from body

COLOR USAGE

Professional Palette
- Primary color for headers and emphasis
- Secondary color for charts and callouts
- Limit to three to four colors
- Ensure accessibility (colorblind-safe)
- Maintain contrast for readability

DOCUMENT LENGTH GUIDELINES

Executive Summary: One to two pages maximum
Business Case: 15 to 25 pages plus appendices
Implementation Roadmap: 20 to 30 pages plus appendices
Assessment Report: 20 to 40 pages plus appendices

Length Principles
- Quality over quantity
- Every page must add value
- Move detail to appendices
- Respect reader time
