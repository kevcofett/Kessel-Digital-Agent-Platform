AUTOMOTIVE VERTICAL OVERLAY v1

PURPOSE

This document provides industry-specific guidance for automotive campaigns. Reference when planning campaigns for OEMs, dealer groups, or automotive aftermarket.

AUTOMOTIVE MEDIA LANDSCAPE

INDUSTRY STRUCTURE

The automotive industry has a unique three-tier structure that affects media planning.

Tier 1 - OEM National:
- Manufacturer-funded national campaigns
- Brand building and model launches
- Largest budgets, broadest targeting
- Sets brand positioning and messaging
- Examples: Ford, Toyota, BMW national campaigns

Tier 2 - Regional Dealer Associations:
- Regional dealer group funding
- Market-level campaigns
- Coordinates with Tier 1 messaging
- Sales event focus
- Examples: SoCal Honda Dealers, Tri-State Toyota

Tier 3 - Individual Dealers:
- Individual dealership campaigns
- Local market focus
- Highest conversion intent
- Inventory-specific advertising
- Examples: Bob Smith Ford, Local Toyota dealer

CO-OP FUNDING DYNAMICS

Co-op Structure:
- OEMs provide co-op funds to dealers
- Requirements vary by manufacturer
- Often requires creative approval
- May mandate specific media mix
- Compliance tracking essential

Planning Implications:
- Understand co-op requirements early
- Build compliant creative
- Plan media eligible for co-op
- Track co-op utilization
- Maximize available funds

PURCHASE CYCLE CHARACTERISTICS

Consideration Timeline:
- Active shopping: 2-4 months typical
- Research phase: starts 6+ months out
- Decision phase: final 2-4 weeks
- Transaction: point of sale

Implications:
- Upper funnel investment justified
- Long attribution windows needed
- Sequential messaging effective
- Retargeting windows longer than typical

AUDIENCE STRATEGIES

IN-MARKET TIMING SIGNALS

High-Intent Indicators:
- Auto loan pre-approvals
- Dealer website visits
- Competitive model research
- Service history suggesting replacement
- Lease maturity timing
- Life event triggers (new home, new baby)

Data Sources:
- Third-party intent providers (Oracle, Polk)
- OEM CRM and loyalty data
- Dealer website behavior
- Search query analysis
- Social signals

Timing Strategy:
- Early funnel: 6-3 months out
- Mid funnel: 3-1 month out
- Lower funnel: final month
- In-market now: active shoppers

CONQUEST VS LOYALTY

Conquest Targeting:
- Competitive vehicle owners
- Competitive model researchers
- Brand switcher profiles
- Dissatisfaction signals

Conquest Tactics:
- Competitive keyword bidding
- Lookalike from conquest buyers
- Competitive conquest audiences
- Model comparison content

Loyalty Targeting:
- Current owners approaching replacement
- Service customers
- Lease maturity audiences
- Brand enthusiast communities

Loyalty Tactics:
- Owner exclusive offers
- Trade-in value messaging
- Model upgrade paths
- Service to sales integration

Balance Framework:
- New model launch: 60-70% conquest focus
- Mature model: 40-50% conquest focus
- Brand under pressure: 70-80% loyalty defense
- Market share growth: 60%+ conquest

SERVICE AND PARTS LIFECYCLE

Service Media Opportunities:
- Maintenance interval reminders
- Recall and safety campaigns
- Seasonal service promotions
- Accessory and parts upsell

Lifecycle Integration:
- Service customers as sales prospects
- Parts buyers as loyalty signals
- Warranty expiration timing
- Accessory purchase triggers

CHANNEL PRIORITIES

SEARCH - BRAND DEFENSE CRITICAL

Search Strategy:
- Brand defense is non-negotiable
- Competitors will bid on your brand terms
- Protect dealer name searches
- Model-specific term coverage
- Maintain high impression share

Budget Allocation:
- Brand terms: 30-40% of search budget
- Model terms: 30-40% of search budget
- Non-brand/category: 20-30% of search budget

Benchmarks:
- Brand CPC: 1-3 dollars
- Model CPC: 2-5 dollars
- Category CPC: 5-15 dollars
- Conversion rate: 3-8%

VIDEO FOR AWARENESS

Video Role:
- Primary vehicle for brand storytelling
- Model feature demonstration
- Emotional connection building
- Launch vehicle for new models

Channel Mix:
- YouTube: reach and targeting efficiency
- CTV: premium, high-attention
- OTT: cord-cutter reach
- Social video: engagement and sharing

Creative Requirements:
- 15-second for awareness frequency
- 30-second for storytelling
- Model-specific vs brand creative
- Dealer tag integration for Tier 2/3

Benchmarks:
- VCR: 70-80%
- CPM: 15-30 dollars (premium video)
- Brand lift: 5-10% awareness increase
- View-through attribution: essential

LOCAL AND GEO TARGETING

Geo Strategy:
- DMA level for Tier 2
- Zip code level for Tier 3
- Radius around dealerships
- Competitive dealer proximity

Local Tactics:
- Google Local campaigns
- Yelp and directory presence
- Local social targeting
- Programmatic geo-fencing

Inventory-Based Targeting:
- Promote available inventory
- Dynamic creative by stock
- Suppress sold vehicles
- Highlight incoming models

DEALER COORDINATION

TERRITORY MANAGEMENT

Territory Considerations:
- Defined dealer territories
- Primary market areas (PMA)
- Secondary market reach
- Cross-territory attribution

Digital Territory Challenges:
- Digital does not respect boundaries
- Attribution across territories
- Lead distribution fairness
- Budget allocation equity

Best Practices:
- Clear territory definitions
- Attribution rules agreed upfront
- Shared market campaigns
- Transparent reporting

INVENTORY-BASED TARGETING

Dynamic Inventory Ads:
- Feed-based creative generation
- Real-time inventory updates
- Price and offer integration
- VIN-specific landing pages

Implementation Requirements:
- Inventory feed integration
- Daily or real-time sync
- Creative template design
- Landing page infrastructure

Performance Impact:
- Higher relevance and CTR
- Improved conversion rates
- Reduced waste on sold inventory
- Better user experience

LEAD DISTRIBUTION

Lead Flow:
- Centralized lead capture
- CRM integration
- Distribution rules by territory
- Response time requirements

Quality Metrics:
- Lead to appointment rate
- Appointment to visit rate
- Visit to sale rate
- Lead source ROI

AUTOMOTIVE MEASUREMENT

SHOWROOM VISIT ATTRIBUTION

Measurement Approaches:
- Mobile location tracking
- Beacon-based measurement
- CRM match with foot traffic
- Survey-based recall

Platform Capabilities:
- Google Store Visits
- Facebook Offline Conversions
- Third-party location providers
- Dealer CRM integration

Attribution Windows:
- 30-day typical for visit attribution
- Longer windows for consideration channels
- Model-specific adjustment needed

TEST DRIVE ATTRIBUTION

Measurement Challenge:
- Test drives happen at dealer
- Digital attribution disconnect
- CRM integration required
- Survey or beacon capture

Proxy Metrics:
- Appointment requests
- Directions clicks
- Call tracking
- Live chat engagement

SALES MATCH BACK

Gold Standard Measurement:
Match ad exposure to actual vehicle sales using registration data.

Implementation:
- Clean room for data matching
- Third-party providers (Polk, Experian)
- OEM registration data access
- Dealer DMS integration

Metrics:
- Cost per vehicle sold
- Sales by model and trim
- Conquest rate from competitive
- Conquest and loyalty split

AUTOMOTIVE BUDGET ALLOCATION

BY FUNNEL STAGE

Awareness (Tier 1 focus):
- Video: 30-40%
- Display: 15-25%
- Social: 15-20%
- Audio: 5-10%

Consideration (All tiers):
- Video: 20-30%
- Search: 25-35%
- Display: 15-25%
- Social: 15-20%

Conversion (Tier 2/3 focus):
- Search: 40-50%
- Display/retargeting: 20-30%
- Social: 15-20%
- Local: 10-15%

SEASONAL PATTERNS

Peak Selling Seasons:
- Spring (March-May): strong selling
- Summer (June-August): moderate
- Fall (September-November): model year end
- Year-end (December): clearance and tax planning

Budget Phasing:
- Increase 20-30% during peak seasons
- Pull back during slow periods
- Save budget for model launches
- Align with OEM incentive timing

Model Year Timing:
- New model launch: heavy investment
- Mid-cycle: steady state
- Model year end: clearance focus
- Refresh/redesign: relaunch investment
