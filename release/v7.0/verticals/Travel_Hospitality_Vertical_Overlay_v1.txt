PURPOSE

This document provides industry-specific guidance for travel and hospitality campaigns. Reference when planning campaigns for hotels, airlines, cruise lines, or travel services.

TRAVEL MEDIA LANDSCAPE

INDUSTRY DYNAMICS

The travel industry has unique competitive dynamics that shape media strategy.

Direct vs OTA:
- Online Travel Agencies (Expedia, Booking.com) compete for bookings
- Direct channels preferred for margin and customer relationship
- OTA billboard effect creates awareness driving direct search
- Balance between distribution and direct acquisition

Key OTAs:
- Expedia Group: Expedia, Hotels.com, Vrbo
- Booking Holdings: Booking.com, Priceline, Kayak
- Airbnb: Alternative accommodations
- TripAdvisor: Reviews and metasearch

Metasearch Importance:
- Google Hotel Ads and Flights
- Trivago, Kayak, TripAdvisor
- Price comparison shopping behavior
- Critical for visibility at decision point

Loyalty Program Integration:
- Loyalty members book direct more often
- Higher lifetime value
- Lower acquisition cost
- Status and benefits drive behavior

COMPETITIVE PRESSURE

OTA Bid Pressure:
- OTAs bid aggressively on brand terms
- Defend brand search essential
- OTA commissions often exceed direct CAC
- Strategic approach to coexistence

Rate Parity Considerations:
- Rate parity clauses with OTAs
- Limits direct pricing advantages
- Bundle and loyalty workarounds
- Value-add differentiation

BOOKING WINDOWS

ADVANCE PURCHASE PATTERNS

Leisure Travel:
- Domestic: 2-6 weeks advance booking
- International: 6-12 weeks advance booking
- Holiday periods: 2-3 months advance
- Peak summer: 3-4 months advance

Business Travel:
- Much shorter booking windows
- Often 1-2 weeks advance
- Day-of and next-day bookings
- Corporate rate visibility

Cruise Industry:
- Wave season (January-March) critical
- 6-12 month advance for cruises
- Last-minute for close-in dates
- Deposit-based booking cycle

LAST-MINUTE OPPORTUNITIES

Last-Minute Segment:
- Growing segment, especially mobile
- Price-sensitive but impulsive
- Higher urgency messaging effective
- Inventory-based targeting valuable

Tactics:
- Mobile-focused campaigns
- Urgency creative messaging
- Inventory availability ads
- App push notification integration
- Geo-targeted near airports

SEASONAL CONSIDERATIONS

Peak Seasons:
- Summer (June-August) for leisure
- Holiday periods (Thanksgiving, Christmas)
- Spring break (March)
- Shoulder season opportunities

Off-Peak Strategy:
- Lower CPCs available
- Promotional pricing
- Extended stay offers
- Business travel focus

Wave Season (Cruise):
- January through March
- Heavy cruise investment
- Competitive bidding intense
- Early booking incentives

CHANNEL PRIORITIES

SEARCH - BRAND BIDDING CRITICAL

Brand Defense:
- OTAs will bid on your brand terms
- Must maintain visibility
- High cost but non-negotiable
- Optimize for efficiency, not elimination

Strategy by Query Type:
- Brand only: Defend aggressively
- Brand + destination: High priority
- Brand + dates: Convert these users
- Generic destination: Compete selectively

Metasearch Integration:
- Google Hotel Ads / Flights
- Trivago and Kayak participation
- Rate and availability accuracy
- Commission vs CPC models

Search Benchmarks:
- Brand CPC: 0.50-2.00 dollars
- Destination CPC: 2-8 dollars
- Generic CPC: 5-15 dollars
- Conversion rate: 2-5%

METASEARCH AND COMPARISON SHOPPING

Metasearch Strategy:

- Inventory accuracy critical

Platform Priorities:

Bidding Approach:

TRAVEL MEASUREMENT

BOOKING ATTRIBUTION

Attribution Challenges:
- Long consideration windows
- Multi-device journeys
- OTA research to direct booking
- Call center bookings

Attribution Windows:
- Click: 14-30 days recommended
- View: 7-14 days for awareness
- Longer for cruise and luxury

Cross-Device Consideration:
- Mobile research, desktop booking
- Device graph integration
- User-based attribution
- Accept some measurement gap

REVENUE PER BOOKING

Primary KPI:
Revenue per booking captures value better than booking count.

Calculation:
Revenue Per Booking = Total Revenue / Number of Bookings

Factors Affecting Revenue:
- Length of stay
- Room type / cabin class
- Ancillary purchases
- F&B and amenities
- Loyalty status

Optimization Target:
- Optimize for revenue, not just bookings
- Higher-value segments worth higher CAC
- LTV-based bidding where possible

ANCILLARY REVENUE

Ancillary Opportunities:
- Room upgrades
- Early check-in / late checkout
- Dining packages
- Spa and activities
- Transportation

Media Implications:
- Value messaging beyond rate
- Bundle promotion
- Post-booking upsell
- Loyalty incentive integration

COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS

OTA BID PRESSURE MANAGEMENT

Strategic Response:

- Focus on efficiency metrics

Tactical Approaches:

Workarounds:

- Package bundling
- Value-add inclusion

Messaging Strategy:

- Book direct advantages
- Loyalty program value
